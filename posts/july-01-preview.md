## Round of 32 Preview

## England vs Congo DR

England and the DRC make their way to Atlanta to start off our Wednesday games. We have already seen England struggle against teams that want to sit back and play counter-attacking football. Additionally, we have seen the Leopards execute at a high level against all three teams they played and take a point off Portugal. Their win chances are being severely underestimated — especially considering what we have already seen from both sides at this tournament.

There is no denying that England are more talented on a team and individual level, but we aren't playing 11 different 1v1s. Tuchel needs to find the right 11 to break down a very stout, compact, and organized Congolese side that has caused problems for every team they have faced. They have only given up one goal a game and scored in two of their three matches. In qualifying, they drew with Senegal, fought to a close 2–3 loss against them, then beat Cameroon, Nigeria, and eventually Jamaica to claim their spot through a playoff gauntlet. They have been playing win-or-go-home football for a full year and will not be intimidated by the moment. Both Yoane Wissa and Aaron Wan-Bissaka play in the Premier League and know the English team and players very well. This will not be easy for England.

That being said, the Three Lions are extremely strong on an individual level — speed, skill, creativity, vision, and discipline. They win the ball back effectively, and all 11 players defend when they need to. I liked Tuchel's decision to play two attacking midfielders with Morgan Rogers and Jude Bellingham together, and I expect something similar here. This game will be a dogfight between two teams whose mascots are cats. All three results are on the table, but I think the Lions prove why they are kings of the jungle.

**Prediction: England 2–1 after extra time**

## Belgium vs Senegal

The Emerald City is the backdrop for game two, as Belgium's reward for winning their group is facing Senegal, the champions of Africa. Two top-20 teams going head-to-head for a spot in the last 16.

For Senegal, they come in as the only team to advance out of the group stage on just 3 points. However, they were competitive in all three games and had to play both France and Norway. They have a lot of momentum, with their last game a 5–0 win over Iraq. We haven't really seen it yet, but this squad is teeming with talent all over the pitch. They navigated their way to the AFCON final — a federation that saw 9 of its 10 teams advance to the Round of 32. Their main star, Sadio Mané, has not been as impactful as hoped, but it only takes one game, and the pieces around him have been getting better. There is young, emerging talent and older, experienced talent coming together, and this is likely Senegal's best window for a deep run in the next two or three cycles.

Belgium come in having had a similar slow start — a couple of draws before dismantling the worst team in their group. Their golden generation is fading, with only a few players left from when they were ranked number one in the world. Their best player is one of the younger options in Jérémy Doku. Unfortunately, I believe this is Doku's last game of the tournament no matter what — he and his wife are welcoming their first child into the world, and understandably, he would like to be there. This game comes down to whether Doku can get enough high-leverage touches on the ball. Part of this needs to come from De Bruyne and Tielemans in distribution, but part of it is what he can create for himself, because he is good enough. For Senegal, they'll need to use their pace and strength to their advantage.

All three options are on the table, but this feels like one that will be settled in normal time.

**Prediction: Senegal 3–2**

## United States vs Bosnia-Herzegovina

The final game of the day is hosted in San Francisco as the Stars and Stripes wrap up Canada Day against the Bosnians. One of the more lopsided matchups of the day.

Bosnia played their group stage to near perfection. They were nearly the better team against Canada in game one, held Switzerland to 0 goals for 70 minutes, and then cleaned up against Qatar in the final game. They have shown their cards: sit back and defend — which they are comfortable doing — and score off set pieces from their limited possession. They can counter, but their pace is lacking, so that is much less of a threat.

For the United States, they wrapped up the group win in two games and rested most of their starters in the final game. The good news is that Pulisic got minutes and looked like himself. The US has been fortunate enough to score in the first 10 minutes of each of their three games. In games against low blocks, scoring early takes a tremendous amount of pressure out of the situation — the game becomes much, much easier. In both of their first two games, it came from getting to the endline with their most dynamic players and forcing own goals. Bosnia is the slowest team the US has faced and also the lowest ranked. The earlier the US scores, the bigger the gap will be. If Bosnia are to win, they will need a piece of magic — as they have managed in two of their five tournament goals.

**Prediction: USA 3–0**
