## Round of 32 Preview

## Spain vs Austria

Los Angeles welcomes Spain and Austria to start the penultimate day of the round of 32. It is a matchup where Spain is heavily favored to win, though we have already seen them falter against Cabo Verde in the group stage. The problem for Austria—or rather, one of many problems—is that Lamine Yamal should be playing the entire game, which he has yet to do in this World Cup. Spain is famous for their midfielders, and this team is no different; there are plenty of players left off the starting squad who would be starting for other teams in this tournament.

Spain will dominate possession as they have in all of their games to this point—the question is whether they will be able to break through. With so many creative playmakers on the pitch and on the bench, you have to think this should be no issue. They managed five goals in a weak group, but four of those came against Saudi Arabia (with three in the first half), and the second goal was actually an own goal. I am a little nervous about their ability to find the breakthrough, as Spain's Achilles' heel, as always, is their lack of a pure, out-and-out striker. Since winning the World Cup in 2010, Spain has not made it past the round of 16, losing to Morocco on penalties in 2022, Russia on penalties in 2018, and famously failing to make it out of the group stage in 2014. Even in their 2010 winning run, they won every knockout game 1-0.

But Austria is not a very strong side, looking somewhat pathetic barring portions of their last game against Algeria. They are coming off an emotional rollercoaster of a game where, in the last five minutes, they went from second place, to out of the tournament, and back to second in the group. They do have solid defenders who should keep this manageable. Their big issue is that they need to play more like Bosnia than Australia to try and score, because they lack pace up front. This is a problem for a couple of reasons: first, we have seen quality from Bosnia already but not really from Austria (even though the talent should be there); second, Spain is far more organized at the back than the US, remaining one of the best teams in the world.

So how can Austria win? It is going to be incredibly difficult, and they will almost certainly need to score multiple goals. If this is going to happen from something other than mistakes or flukes, it must come down to Marcel Sabitzer carrying the team, creating opportunities for himself and his teammates. We know he is an excellent player, but Austria is asking him to play a bit out of position compared to his club role (as they should). Spain cruises in a 2-0 snoozefest, slowly choking out the game and denying Austria any real opportunities.

**Prediction: Spain 2-0**

## Portugal vs Croatia

The final Europe-on-Europe action in the round of 32 sees Portugal face off against Croatia in a battle of legends, Ballon d'Or winners, former teammates, and magnificent midfields. Croatia has reached the semifinals in the last two World Cups, losing to France in the 2018 final and Argentina in the 2022 semifinals. Even in 1998, their first World Cup as an independent country, they managed a third-place finish. In fact, they have only missed the 2010 World Cup since gaining independence, and whenever they make it out of the group stage, they have never finished worse than third.

They seem to have grown into the tournament; playing one of the best teams in the tournament first was not ideal, but they still produced some real quality in that game. They have a wealth of technicians in midfield, which they will need when going up against a Portuguese midfield that rivals the Spanish in terms of depth and talent. Do Perišić and Modrić have enough left in the tank?

As for Portugal, we are still waiting to see their talent on full display. They seemingly always enter these large tournaments with one of the best rosters on paper, yet they have failed to make any real noise other than winning Euro 2016 in what was an underwhelming performance for a champion. Funnily enough, in the first knockout round of that tournament, they beat Croatia with a late extra-time goal. These two sides have seen quite a bit of each other lately, with five matchups since 2020: one friendly and four in the UEFA Nations League. Portugal has gone 3-1-1 in those games, carrying a slight psychological advantage.

For Portugal to win, they need their midfielders to get going and exert absolute dominance over the game. They have plenty of different profiles—from control and creativity to ball-winning—but we have yet to see the chemistry click. Both teams have struggled a bit at the back relative to their talent level. One of Croatia's best players, Joško Gvardiol, has not had a good tournament and was relegated to the bench in their last game against Ghana.

To me, that is what this game comes down to: which defense is able to step up. I am not worried about either midfield, nor am I worried about Modrić or Ronaldo. These players know how to rise to the occasion. Both legends likely understand that this is it; both are near or over 40, neither is having their best tournament, and four more years is a long time to maintain this level of play. Can the Croatian defense stop looking like Swiss cheese? Can the Portuguese defense apply pressure on the midfield and on the flanks the way their individual stars do for their club teams? We shall see.

The wildcard here is Martin Baturina. If he has a coming-out party and a "passing of the torch" game from Modrić, Croatia can win. Ultimately, all three results are on the table as 8th meets 13th here in the round of 32. I will go with recent history and the team with more depth: Portugal 2-1 in extra time. One final note: both teams have taken a ton of penalties in major tournaments, so a shootout does not favor either side.

**Prediction: Portugal 2-1 after extra time**

## Switzerland vs Algeria

After winning against Canada, the Swiss had the luxury of staying in Vancouver to welcome the Algerians after their last-second draw against Austria. By drawing that game, Algeria gets to play Switzerland instead of Spain, which was ultimately a win. It was a draw that saw them move from second to third place (like a loss) but set them up against a weaker opponent (which feels more like a win).

So far, Switzerland has been a highly inconsistent team. They have looked excellent for large stretches of all three matches, but they have not looked dangerous enough consistently to be truly dominant. There were parts of each game where I thought they were going to win 5-0, but there were also moments where they looked disorganized, unoriginal, and surprisingly panicked. Veteran captain Granit Xhaka should have the boys ready to go and calm under pressure in a game they are expected to win.

We saw Marco Manzambi start in their last game against Canada, and I expect we will see the same here. He looked good for the 85 minutes he played, remaining explosive and creative throughout. We have yet to see a quintessential Xhaka moment, which we seemingly get at almost every tournament. The Swiss are one of the most comfortable teams on the ball, but they are also one of the most uncomfortable without it. When they had to defend against both Bosnia and Canada, they conceded goals; the defensive display against Canada, in particular, looked chaotic. Granted, both goals conceded were world-class, but the performance still does not inspire confidence.

As for Algeria, they come into the game with significantly less rest than the Swiss. They also had a much tougher group stage, having to play Argentina alongside an even matchup against Austria. To be honest, this team hasn't looked great in the tournament, perhaps looking only slightly better than Austria despite a heavier loss to Argentina and a narrower win against Jordan. Captain Riyad Mahrez has started the last two games, which seems to have made a difference. I have been particularly impressed with 20-year-old Ibrahim Maza; he has looked dynamic and creative on the ball while still holding possession like a veteran defensive midfielder.

I have harped on this repeatedly, but when you put these young players on the roster, they must play—and I would like to see them start. If they have the talent, get them on the field; otherwise, they are just taking up a roster spot. Between Maza and Anis Hadj Moussa anchoring a young core, I expect we will see Algeria at several World Cups to come, and they are nowhere near finished here.

I expect them to sit back and invite Swiss pressure. Algeria certainly has the pace and skill on the flanks to execute a counterattack. This will be a clash of styles in a match that is highly representative of the tournament we have seen so far. Algeria is not the most organized or well-known team for their defensive structure, and for that reason, I favor the Swiss. I can absolutely see an upset by the Fennecs, but I like Switzerland to take it in a hard-fought 2-0 win.

**Prediction: Switzerland 2-0**
