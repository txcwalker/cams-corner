## France vs Senegal

In the first colonization battle of the tournament, it was another tale of two halves. Les Bleus looked Les Mis in the first half—lackadaisical, sloppy, and bored—with Senegal having all of the chances and fun. Truth be told, they should have been up 1-0 or even 2-0 going into the break. Then, out of the break, France came out and looked like the best team in the world. They were moving, creating chances, and acting decisively. They earned their three second-half goals. Mbappé had two of the goals and looked much more lively in the second half. For me, this game was run by two of the French midfielders: Adrien Rabiot, who was great the whole game, and Michael Olise, who set up the first goal and was the most creative player on the field for the French. For the Lions of Senegal, their first half looked excellent. They were containing the French attack, which never looked likely to score, and threatening on the counter, including a shot off the post and another blazed over the bar that should have been buried. They did all of the hard work and then couldn't seal the deal to get a goal on the board. They did eventually score, coming from their 18-year-old sub, on a goal that the French goalkeeper would surely like to have back. Overall, both teams will need to be better moving through the tournament. We did get to see 45 minutes of France flexing, and we saw why Senegal are the champions of Africa—or maybe champions.

## Norway vs Iraq

Erling Haaland got his World Cup account off to a hot start with two goals in a 4-1 win over the Iraqis. He and Norway as a whole looked good and deserved the three points. That being said, they will need to improve to have the same result against Senegal and to even have a chance against France. It was a bit disappointing to see the midfield struggle a bit, or, better put, not have the impact on the game that their talent would suggest. For me, that was their biggest room for improvement. For Iraq, they looked quite good and the scoreline is unfair. While they got outplayed, it was not by the gap the score would suggest. They had a couple of sloppy moments at the back, which Norway capitalized on immediately. But they showed skill, pace, and—most impressively—confidence, especially in the attacking zone. Unfortunately for them, this was supposed to be their easiest game, and there is no respite with Senegal and France to come on the schedule. That being said, I will be looking for more from Iraq in the future.

## Argentina vs Algeria

So this Messi guy is pretty good, huh? He was apparently nursing an injury of sorts, but he looked just fine to me. Argentina had quite an easy time disposing of Algeria 3-0 in a game that was never really in doubt. Of course Messi looked good and scored a hat-trick, but the Algerian defense was shockingly bad on all three goals. On the first goal, we had a 20-yard pass go through five guys, and the goalkeeper got both hands on the ball but couldn't keep it out. The second goal came from a rebound that probably should have been smothered by the goalkeeper, and the final goal featured some of the most pathetic defending at the World Cup, as his shot went between five defenders and the goalkeeper could do nothing about it. Obviously, he still had to be in those positions and execute, but let's not pretend like he did all of the work for these goals. The attacking prowess was on full display for the white and sky-blue tonight. As for the Fennecs, they showed some promise attacking and even scored a goal that was called back due to a close offside call. I can't say they played well because there were too many defensive lapses in concentration, and Luca Zidane (son of Zinedine Zidane, one of the GOATs whom some of you may know better as the headbutter in 2006) had an up-and-down night. There were too many downs, though, and we will see if he starts again in their next match. They have a lot to improve, but their games get much, much easier facing both Jordan and Austria in this group.

## Austria vs Jordan

Our final game took us to Santa Clara where the peppermint patties of Austria faced off against the Al-Nashama (The Noble/Chivalrous) of Jordan. Austria ended up with the win in a scoreline that does not accurately reflect the game: 3-1. It feels weird to say, but I think Jordan was the better team, with the major exception that their goalkeeper had a horrible night and, I assume, will be replaced for their next match. Losing here is sort of a kiss of death in this group, unless they can somehow manage to steal a point against Argentina. If they are going to advance, they'll need to do it on three points, which is a long shot. Still, they have a lot to be proud of and build on; they are going to give Algeria everything they can handle and a bit more. As for Austria, this was not a good performance. They were sloppy for the first 90 minutes of the game, though they did show a bit of class playing keep-away for the last 10 minutes, finishing with a goal from a penalty. We are going to need to see the closing-moments version of Austria in the rest of their games for them to advance out of the group, let alone win a knockout stage game. On a random note, this game had only about 30 seconds of active play between the 60th and 73rd minutes, resulting in 10 minutes of stoppage time—the longest of the tournament so far.
