## First Round Review

Each team has played once and we have seen the form of all the teams. Let's start with the good. In no particular order, these have been the teams that stood out in a positive way the most to me: Germany, the US, Australia, France and England. Picking between the five, England probably looked the best. If it were not for some real moments of quality and a goalkeeper going crazy for Croatia, this game could have easily been 6–0. I do want to touch on Australia quickly as well. They not only looked great, but they are also one of the biggest surprises of the tournament. They play a very patient counter-attacking style and are blazing fast up top, which is a big problem for most of the teams at the tournament. There are zero easy games at this tournament, which is much different than previous tournaments, even the most recent World Cup four years ago.

On the other side, the teams that have looked the worst so far have been South Africa, Tunisia, all of Group B (Switzerland, Canada, Qatar and Bosnia), Paraguay and Czechia. With South Africa being the worst by a long shot, they are followed by Tunisia, who had their manager fired immediately following their first game. None of these matches will be easy, but these teams have all looked like some combination of sloppy, uninspired, indecisive and slow. I mean that in gameplay style, not in actual speed.

The most surprising teams have been Ivory Coast, New Zealand, Spain, Cabo Verde, Iraq and Jordan. Some might say both Ghana and the DRC are surprising as well, but I expected them to be competitive based on their overall talent level and group dynamics. Most of these are positive surprises, with the exception of Spain. They looked like they badly, badly needed Lamine Yamal on the field. It was way too stagnant and passive for them. New Zealand came into the tournament as the lowest ranked team, and after watching that game against a very good Iran team, you cannot tell me that is an accurate statement. Those rankings are on watch, and depending on how the rest of the group stage games go, we can just throw them out the window. I did call out Iraq and Jordan specifically, but I thought as a region Asia looked really good and maybe the best front to back. I was pleasantly surprised by the weaker teams, and the stronger teams all either won or drew their games. While Iraq, Jordan and Uzbekistan all lost, the scorelines were not representative of the play, with all three teams giving up goals in various bizarre ways on the last touch of the game. So expect this region to challenge in the rest of the tournament and in future tournaments.

## Portugal vs Congo DR

Alright, let's get into the games. We talked a bit about this in the preview, but Portugal can struggle to put the ball in the back of the net at major tournaments. That was unfortunately on display in their 1–1 draw against the DRC. Let's not take anything away from Les Léopards; they might have been the better team on the pitch, but there is no disputing who has more talent. The team is going to have to have a Ronaldo conversation. To me, he looked good in all aspects of the game except the physical ones. He was just a little too slow. He knew what he wanted to do and was supposed to do but was just a millisecond late. I think he needs to come on early in the second half so he can play the game at 100 percent while he is on the field and not try to manage his energy. Still, he has plenty in the tank for the whole game. With just a bit more composure in the final third, the DRC could have won this game. It looks promising moving forward. They will need Wissa to continue to create space and lead from the front to move through the tournament.

## England vs Croatia

This was the game that everyone had circled on their calendar when the draws initially came out. Ultimately, England overpowered Croatia 4–2 in one of the more exciting games of the tournament up until this point. The first half was an incredible back and forth affair where England took advantage of a couple of egregious Croatian mistakes. But Croatia was able to level it up after both goals with some sublime moments of quality, particularly on the second goal just before halftime. That will be in contention for goal and assist of the tournament. I cannot begin to describe how difficult that backward header is to see, let alone execute. It is easily the best header in the World Cup since the Van Persie Flying Dutchman in 2014, and I would argue it is better. As for the second half, England showed their depth and talent. From pretty much the onset, it was all England, and there was a 15-minute period where it felt like England should have scored three or four times. The Croatian goalie was the best player on the field, which is weird given that England scored four times, but his defense did him no favors today. Croatia had a few chances, but they were half chances at best and would have required the magic of the goals scored in the first half, and that bottle was in a bit of debt. If you are rooting for the Vatreni, do not panic. Their best player, Luka Modrić, had his worst World Cup game ever, and he is playing in his fifth World Cup at 40 years old. The rest of the offense and midfield looked pretty good, and they depend on him to make plays and distribute the ball. His club season was good this year as he was one of the best players in Serie A, and I would write this off as a bad day and an anomaly, not the new norm. If he looks this bad again, then you can start to panic.

## Ghana vs Panama

The other side of this group saw the Black Stars of Ghana find a late winner against Los Canaleros of Panama. This was a strange game through and through. Panama looked like the better team early in the game, but throughout the contest, they never looked like they were going to score. Towards the end of the game, it felt like Ghana was the only team that could score, and they did eventually get a goal in transition late into stoppage time. Both teams are going to have issues moving forward. For Panama, they need something more incisive and direct in the final third. They had no flair and no skill expression in that aspect of the game. For Ghana, they need to get Antoine Semenyo on the ball more. Whether that means having him drift into the middle a bit or coming back for the ball, I am not sure. He is their best player and one of the best players in the tournament, and for me, he did not touch the ball enough. I will say I was thoroughly impressed with Brandon Thomas-Asante, who wears the number 10 for Ghana. I am sure the rest of the world is familiar with him, but he looked excellent, finding space well and creating for his teammates as a number 10 needs to in any game, but especially this tournament. He plays his club football for Coventry City, who just earned promotion to the Premier League after this season. He had a nice season, scoring 13 times (tied for second on the team) and assisting on four more. Look for him to help lead this team alongside Semenyo.

## Uzbekistan vs Colombia

Our final game of the round one group stage phase took us back to where we started in Mexico City as the White Wolves of Uzbekistan took on the Colombian Coffee Growers. As I mentioned earlier, I think the scoreline is a bit unfair to the Uzbeks, who gave up a late goal at the death of the game. That being said, Colombia did pressure for a huge portion of the game and had plenty of high quality chances. To me, this game was perfectly encapsulated by the last two minutes of the match. Uzbekistan was trailing 2–1 with a few minutes left in stoppage time and picked up a foul for a free kick in a dangerous attacking area. The service all night had been nothing short of awful, with free kicks being kicked out of bounds with no chance of anyone touching them, and the same thing happened here even though they changed free kick takers. Then the ball was moved down the field by Colombia and one of their subs put in an inch-perfect cross, which was converted for a headed goal. He could not have run the ball over and put it in a better spot. That was the difference in the game and, to some extent, the difference between the powerhouse teams and the weaker ones. All of the teams have the ability to execute at a high level and they all have reasonable tactics and game plans, which has not always been the case. But these high-level teams are able to do it at a more consistent level. You might only get a few chances or half chances a game, and you must, must, must make the most of them. For Colombia, their big name players played okay. They are going to need more from Luis Díaz and James Rodríguez. They have talent, but not quite as much as the rest of the top tier teams, so they will need their all stars to be all world in this tournament. They have put themselves in a good position to advance, but they need to keep their foot on the gas as their next two games will be much more difficult. That is not to say Uzbekistan played poorly. My goodness, first World Cup, first game, first goal. There are teams that have played entire World Cups and not scored at all. They should be proud of their performance on a macro level, but on a micro level, they put themselves in a position to challenge for a point right up to the end of the game and just could not convert.
