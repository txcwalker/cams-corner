## United States vs Australia

The game of the day is the first one on the docket as Seattle welcomes the US and Australia in a face-off between two game-one winners. Both teams were equally dominant in their wins — just in completely different ways. The US smashed Paraguay in very traditional fashion, moving the ball around, finding defensive holes, and showing some nice individual skill. Australia completely stifled Türkiye with a defensive shell and counter-attack tactics, showing pace and organizational dominance throughout.

In a way, this is going to look a lot like the Australia–Türkiye game. The US certainly has more talent, and Australia will happily sit back and absorb pressure, looking to use their pace up top to punish transition opportunities on the counter. The US will have more possession, but that is completely meaningless without decisive finishing and playmaking. They need to find a way through the Socceroos for the game to open up. For Australia, this goes wrong if the US scores early — one is tolerable, but a second goal is a death blow. Overall, this looks like one of the more even matchups of the World Cup so far, as two contrasting styles meet in Group D.

## Scotland vs Morocco

Game two sees Scotland stay put in Boston as Morocco takes the train up from New York City. Before anyone asks, I did speak with Michelle Wu and she has assured me the city has plenty of beer for the Tartan Army.

This is a super interesting match. While Morocco looks much better on paper, this Scottish side has a fair amount of individual skill. Expect the Kilts to sit back, allow Morocco to have the ball, and look to score in transition — but they'll also try to take the air out of the game with some spurts of possession. They have the players to play keep-away for at least a bit. For the Atlas Lions, they'll be a bit frustrated not to have three points already after outplaying Brazil in game one. A repeat performance is probably enough to win here, and that's what I'm expecting. Early goals will put pressure on Scotland, who will be desperate for any kind of point so they don't go into their final match against Brazil needing a result. I think there is just too much talent in the Moroccan midfield for Scotland to compete. Expect a relatively comfortable win for the Atlas Lions.

## Brazil vs Haiti

Game three sees our first 7:30 PM local kickoff of the tournament, as Brazil and Haiti take the train down to Philadelphia for what is an all-but must-win game for both sides. Brazil comes in as massive favorites, but if they play like they did against Morocco, Haiti has more than a chance.

Vini Jr. was outstanding in game one, and then there were two or three empty levels of performance before the rest of the Brazil team, who were scattered through the B and C tiers of play. This is a classic get-right game, and don't be surprised to see them pour it on if they get going early. They need to build confidence heading into their last group game against Scotland and a probable knockout round appearance. I'm specifically looking for improvement from Igor Thiago up top and Lucas Paquetá in midfield. I'm also curious whether the manager will swap the formation or personnel to get some different players on the field — my expectation is we see Matheus Cunha starting in place of one of those two. Additionally, there are two 19-year-olds on the roster in Rayan and Endrick. This seems like the perfect game to introduce at least one of them to the world stage — I'd love to see both, even as subs. And lastly, I want to see more from Raphinha. He can absolutely take over games and is the perfect balance to Vinícius Júnior on the opposite side of the pitch.

As for Haiti, they are going to have to defend for 90 minutes. Gone are the days of the local dentist flying in a few hours before kickoff. These are all professional players, including Premier League and Ligue 1 talent. If they get chances on the counter, look for Jean Ricner Bellegarde and Wilson Isidor to convert — they have the talent to take their chances. I'm expecting a confidence-building win for Brazil, with both Raphinha and Cunha having big games, and I hope we get to see one of those 19-year-olds. We do not bring these guys to the World Cup to sit on the bench. For Haiti, if they have any chance, they must, must, must be patient and take two or three of the chances they get throughout the game.

## Türkiye vs Paraguay

Our last game of the night takes us back out to the West Coast as Türkiye takes on Paraguay in the city by the bay. Well, really the city by the city by the bay. Whatever. This is a must-win game for both teams if they want to play knockout football.

For the Turks, they need their stars to show up. They got them on the ball plenty in their loss to Australia, but they couldn't do anything with it. They can't do it alone though, and will need more from the supporting cast. For Paraguay, I wouldn't be shocked to see them take a page from the Socceroos and try to hit back on the counter after Türkiye overextends. They have the pace to do so and maybe even more talent. Their outside players — most notably Miguel Almirón — have the pace to stretch the defense, and Julio Enciso has the talent to play the perfect pass at the right moment to unlock high-leverage transition opportunities.

For the Ay Yıldızlılar, I'm looking at one player: Kenan Yıldız. He plays the same position and style as Christian Pulisic, just for a different high-profile Serie A team. We saw how Pulisic had his way with the left side of the Paraguayan defense in game one. Yıldız might be even better, so I'm looking for him to demand the ball early and start creating chances from the first minute. He is a confident, high-skill player who loves taking on defenders one-on-one or even one-on-two. Recall that the US scored early against Paraguay and it was basically over from there — look for the Turks to be aggressive early and ease some tension. Can Paraguay win this game? Yes, technically. But even more than Brazil–Haiti, this is a get-right game for Türkiye. I expect the Turkish talent to win out in a significant way.
