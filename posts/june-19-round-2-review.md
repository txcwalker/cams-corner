## Group Stage Round 2 Review

A full day of group stage football and somehow, the biggest takeaway is that the gap between the sharks and the minnows is closing — but we aren't there yet. When the smaller teams get their chances now, the problem isn't creating them. It's the final touch.

## Czechia vs South Africa

The first game of round two saw Czechia and South Africa square off in a battle to avoid elimination. And somehow, someway, they likely eliminated each other with a 1–1 draw. This game was nearly a perfect mirror image: Czechia looked great for about five minutes until their goal, then a lot of nothing happened — and I mean a lot — then South Africa converted their penalty and played pretty well for the rest of the game, which was about ten minutes. Both teams had premium chances to win it at the end, and neither could convert.

Two quick points. First, I think South Africa thought they were playing golf for the first hour of the game. Their keeper would boot it 65-plus yards hoping they'd recover on the second or third touch. Then a random player would try to score from wherever they picked up the ball — between 30 and 40 yards from goal. The few moves they had before the 85th minute that actually looked dangerous all came from sustained one-and-two touch passing. It is crazy how when you make easy passes, good things happen. Second, this was correctly called a penalty. You can't have your arm just dangling out there, but it's a gross one to give. The defender was not trying to stop the ball, and he was so close there was no time to react. But like I said, it looked unnatural, and the rules are clear — you cannot extend your arm in the box like that.

Both teams will need a win to advance, and based on how this group is playing out, I expect neither of them will get one. They are each going into their final matches as significant underdogs. I wish we could have seen both teams go for the win much earlier. I know Czechia was winning for 80 minutes, but they could have pressed harder. Now they are going to hold hands all the way to Cancún.

## Switzerland vs Bosnia-Herzegovina

SoFi Stadium hosted the Swiss and the Bosnians in game two of the day. For about an hour, Bosnia held strong. They kept their shape, had a few half chances, and defended well — with one notable exception: containing Dan Ndoye down the left side. He looked like all of Switzerland's offense and has clearly earned his spot in the starting lineup for the final group game. However, Manzambi and Vargas came on late and led the Swiss to a 4–1 victory.

Manzambi in particular was able to find space, create space, and cause overall havoc for the Bosnian backline. He does exactly what you want from an attacking midfielder — making life easier for his teammates and harder for everyone else. His technique on both goals was immaculate. He drew the red card, created several chances that ended in big saves or near misses, and genuinely looked like one of the best players on the field. For Bosnia, their consolation goal was world class. You will not see a ball hit better than that in this World Cup. It was a complete missile, struck by Mahmić with his first touch of the game. The technique to keep it both down and on frame was as good as it gets.

Bosnia is still alive. Their point against Canada gives them some hope going into their final group game against Qatar. They'll need a win — and likely a multi-goal win to chip into goal difference — but four points will put them in contention among the third-place teams. For Switzerland, they'll look to win the group against Canada, but the manager has some real questions to answer about the starting lineup. Manzambi has been electric off the bench twice now. Are you keeping him as a super sub? If so, is he getting more time? If not, who does he start over — Ndoye or Vargas? Both have looked excellent. These are good problems to have. Looking forward to seeing more from Switzerland.

## Canada vs Qatar

Not much to say here. Soccer is tough down one man, let alone two. Even before the first red card, Qatar hardly had a touch in the middle third, let alone the attacking third. Canada opened up on the Qataris 6–0, and Qatar nearly doubled the tournament's total red cards, going down to nine men with 40 minutes still left in the game. Both cards were completely deserved, and the second one caused one of the more brutal injuries we've seen in a while. The players were remorseful, and it looks like Ismaël Koné is going to be okay, but he has a long road ahead. If you haven't seen the clip, do yourself a favor and don't look.

For Canada, I believe they scored the first direct free kick of the tournament, and they now control the group on goal differential heading into their showdown with Switzerland. For Qatar, it is mathematically possible they advance, but it's not looking good. Their lone bright spot was goalkeeper Mahmud Abunada. Despite giving up six goals, he was excellent — debatably the Man of the Match, as absurd as that sounds. He played that well. These are two teams moving in completely opposite directions.

## Mexico vs South Korea

Strong goalkeeping continued in this one as El Tri eked out a 1–0 victory over South Korea. This was a rather disappointing performance from Mexico. They looked great in transition and very bland everywhere else, not playing a traditional counter-attacking style but instead relying on mistakes and turnovers in the midfield to generate chances. They were rewarded by the first big goalkeeper error of the tournament when Kim Seung-gyu gifted them the goal. Outside of that howler, he was actually one of Korea's best players, making big saves on Mexican transition moves. His counterpart on the other end was slightly better — making a couple of crazy saves near the end of the game. For me, he was the Man of the Match, because without him Mexico is at best drawing but probably losing.

This is another game where Mexico won but looked uninspired. Dare I say they were the worse of the two teams. They were dominated in midfield, and execution in the final third was difficult at best. South Korea had more chances, especially late, and they couldn't convert — which has been a running theme this tournament. The finishing has been lacking from a lot of teams, including some of our tournament favorites like Spain and Portugal.

Mexico becomes the first team to mathematically secure advancement to the knockout rounds, but they can still finish second in the group if they lose to Czechia and South Korea wins and covers the goal differential. I doubt we get there, but it's worth noting — the group isn't mathematically locked up yet.
