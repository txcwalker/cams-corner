## Group Stage Round 2 Review

A full slate of Group C and D action, and we came away with a lot of clarity — and a few more questions. Two teams punched their tickets to the knockouts, one team's tournament is already in tatters, and controversy found its home in Foxborough.

## United States vs Australia

In one of the most watched US soccer games ever, the US Men's National Team took on the Socceroos in the Emerald City. The game started very open, with many chances in transition for both teams. Just like in their first game, the US were able to get to the endline and force an own goal out of their opponents for an early 1–0 lead. The game settled into a bit of a rut afterward, playing out very similarly to Australia's first game — where Türkiye had a lot of possession and not a lot to show for it. The US snuck a second goal in just before half, and that was that. It ended 2–0.

It was a bit uninspiring from the US in the second half. They were content to sit back and defend for the majority of it. They had their chances on the counter, which they were not able to convert, and that was a little concerning. Overall though, Australia never looked like they were threatening to score, and the US had an easy time closing the game down.

One thing that was pretty peculiar was some of the choices the Australian manager made with the lineup — taking out their best options on the counter attack and opting for a slower, more defensively minded setup. Then he subbed those players back on in the second half when down two, when there would be far fewer transition opportunities. The US were fortunate to score early, really taking the pressure off, because scoring first against a team that wants to defend for 90 minutes is always tough. For the first time in their history, the US move on to the knockout stage needing only two games to do so. For Australia, this is not the end of the world. They play the weakest team in the group next in Paraguay and will likely advance with only a draw. Their goal differential is zero, so they will be a strong third-place team.

## Scotland vs Morocco

Game two of the day started off with a bang as the Tartan Army welcomed the Atlas Lions to Foxborough, with Morocco able to take a 1–0 win. It took a few games, but we finally have our first properly controversial match of the World Cup. The referee had an awful day. Both teams will say he was worse for them — in reality, Helen Keller would have been better.

Play was allowed to be pretty physical, which is fine, but there was no consistency. You might have reached a point where play was so physical it wasn't even a foul anymore. Scotland had two penalty shouts. The second wasn't it for me, but the first was genuinely questionable — though I think both players went to ground a bit too easily. That said, Morocco are lucky, as many referees would have called one or both. There was also a curious foul in the 23rd minute that looked like a direct red to me, denying a clear and obvious goal-scoring chance as the last defender.

Overall though, Scotland weren't great until about the 85th minute and looked way too slow, with players taking a bit too long to make decisions. They will need to speed up the game to take a point off Brazil. For Morocco, this was concerning. They scored an absolute worldie in the first 60 seconds and then did a lot of nothing. They had plenty of chances in transition but couldn't get most of them on target — and the Scottish goalkeeper had an excellent game that deserves some credit. For the last handful of matches, Morocco have played great, scored, and then looked mediocre at best. They will need to address this moving forward or they are in danger of an early exit. This game showed that there are no free wins in this tournament. You will have to work hard for every point.

## Brazil vs Haiti

Well, we were calling for a get-right game for Brazil, and here it was. They were moving without the ball, finding open space, passing players open, and finishing well enough in front of goal. This was all in the first half. For the second half, I think they were just a smidge too content. While the game was never in doubt, because they drew with Morocco, goal differential matters a ton in this group. I think this is a decision that could come back to bite them. Morocco will push against Haiti now, as they have some goal differential to make up.

Matheus Cunha started up top over Igor Thiago, and it looks like it made a big difference. Brazil did use their oldest lineup since 1970, I believe, with an average age of just over 30. Something concerning is that one of their best players, Raphinha, had to be subbed off in the 38th minute. His health is imperative to their success in this tournament. As for Haiti, they were always just happy to be here — they are the first country to be officially eliminated. This was always going to be the case, and they were overmatched significantly by all three teams in the group. Still, they put up competitive showings in their first two games and could have even drawn game one. I am looking forward to progress in Haitian soccer, but first they need to focus on making improvements at home.

## Türkiye vs Paraguay

Pathetic. Just completely and utterly pathetic. And disappointing. Türkiye lost 1–0 to a Paraguay team that was down to ten men for half the game. I am at a loss for words. I get that Paraguay sat behind the ball for pretty much the entire game, even before the red card. But you have two of the premier passers and playmakers in the world in Arda Güler and Hakan Çalhanoğlu, and one of the most dynamic players on the left side in Kenan Yıldız — and you could figure absolutely nothing out? You had months to prep for both of these teams, knowing their game plan was going to be to sit back in a shell and counter attack.

Expect the coach to be fired essentially immediately after the World Cup, or possibly before they even leave the US. Overall, this is a really sad and disappointing showing from Türkiye. They will play the US B team now that the US has won the group. I expect to see a bunch of young players get some experience. As for Paraguay — technically, they won. They scored in the second minute and played 90 minutes of defense. Getting a player red carded for potentially saying something racist is never a great look. This is a new rule implemented this World Cup where covering your mouth is an automatic red card. The player and team didn't fight it particularly hard, so I imagine something unpleasant was said — but who knows. They still have a chance to advance, but will almost certainly need a win. Australia sit in the driver's seat for the second seed and could even advance with a loss, though probably not. This will be an interesting final game, as two teams that want to defend and play on the counter meet each other.
