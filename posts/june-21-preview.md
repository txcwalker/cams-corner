## Spain vs Saudi Arabia

Two teams moving in completely opposite directions. Saudi Arabia are thrilled to have gotten a point in their first game after barely holding on in the second half against Uruguay. They now face, in theory, a much better team in the Spanish. They are going to need to play 90 minutes of their first-half performance to have a chance here.

I know it is fun to say "Oh, Spain are terrible and they drew Cabo Verde, hahahahaha." And yes, that is funny. But these are the best of the best — one of the few national teams that can compete with many of the top club teams in the world. They will make adjustments and they are going to score goals. There is just way too much talent on this team for anything else to happen. They are not just professionals; they are the players, staff, and coaches who are innovating and pushing the game forward. Expect a much more creative and decisive game plan for La Roja. If Lamine Yamal is not injured or is getting back to fitness, I want to see him for 90 minutes — he is the best player in the world and needs to be starting in every lineup. I expect something in the 5–0 or 6–1 neighborhood. That said, they need the first goal, and really a second, to push Saudi Arabia out of their defensive shell. Most teams will have watched the Cabo Verde tape and have plans on how to break it down. The Green Falcons will look to emulate that and should have no issues on the counter, as their offensive options are solid. The defensive idea is to move like a school of fish — ten players becoming one. If they can do this, they can replicate the Cabo Verde results. Expect big games from pretty much everyone in red.

## Belgium vs Iran

In Group G's first round of games, each team picked up one point, and the team that looked worst relative to their ranking was easily Belgium. Their golden generation is fading out, and if they are going to challenge in this World Cup, they need to right the ship here. Their task is not super easy — they face an Iranian team that is solid and has been consistently improving on the world stage. They have very rarely been an easy out recently. That said, Iran do not pose the same attacking threat that Egypt did, which should make the game a bit easier for Belgium. It changes both teams' game plans, but it doesn't mean Iran will be walked over.

For Iran, they need to play with the same energy they came out with against New Zealand. I won't say they were unlucky not to win, because the Kiwis were excellent — but they played much better than other teams that drew and even a few that won (looking at you, Austria). For Belgium, selfishly, I want to see this group of players move through the tournament and find out if they can compete at the highest level. De Bruyne and Tielemans have been such a joy to watch in the midfield for over a decade, and now with Doku they really do have the firepower to win the tournament — of course, I've said the same thing the past three World Cups. I expect Belgium to edge this one, something like 2–1 or 1–0, but don't be surprised to see Iran earn a draw or even the full three points.

## Uruguay vs Cabo Verde

Most teams will have watched the Spain–Cabo Verde game to understand exactly what happened and how to break it down or improve upon it. No team has studied that game more than Uruguay — or at least, they should have. I expect them to attack much more dynamically than Spain did, and honestly they were on the right track with their second half against Saudi Arabia. I want to see Valverde running this game and putting his wingers in position to break down the Cabo Verde backline.

For Cabo Verde, they are actually already in a decent position to advance as a third-place team, so if they go down a goal or two, expect them to stay in their defensive blocks and look to take their chances against Saudi Arabia in game three. They will need to make adjustments though — they are no longer the underdog or overlooked. Teams are taking them seriously now, and they must continue to improve and innovate on the game plan that allowed them to draw Spain. This is how it goes wrong: if they sit back and expect the exact same ideas to work, they will get smoked. Most of these players are not used to constantly innovating or having a target on their backs, and it is a lot of adjustments to make in a short time. The rest of the world is hoping they can hang — they are playing with house money and then some. I'm still expecting a Uruguay win, but nothing outrageous. Probably 1–0 or 2–0.

## New Zealand vs Egypt

The final game of the day is the one I am most excited about. The All Whites take on the Pharaohs from Egypt. This Egyptian team reminds me a lot of Sweden — their offensive options are the strongest part of the team and they will need to score multiple goals a game to win. Salah and Marmoush will need to put their stamp on not just this game but the tournament if Egypt is to stay competitive. They can definitely do it here, but New Zealand proved they are no joke on the world stage with a 2–2 draw against Iran.

For the Kiwis, I am really hoping their showing was not a one-and-done. They came in as the lowest-rated team in the tournament, and there is no way they are the worst team here. They were one of the few sides not desperately struggling with finishing — both of their goals were beautifully executed by striker Elijah Just. As a team, they will want to play through star Chris Wood and his hold-up play. He is their best player and will demand the attention of often both center-backs, leaving room for teammates running in behind. As unexpected as the Cabo Verde result was, I would argue the New Zealand showing was equally surprising — not because they got a point, but how they looked. They could have easily won game one and I expect them to challenge in this group. Group H is looking hyper-competitive. Any team can finish in any position.
