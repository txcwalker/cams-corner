## Group Stage Round 2 Review

Groups G and H delivered a full day of drama — from a summer of goalkeepers continuing, to Cabo Verde doing Cabo Verde things again, to Egypt flipping a switch at halftime and putting the group in their hands.

## Spain vs Saudi Arabia

We talked about how this was a get-right game for Spain, and they managed to beat the Green Falcons 4–0. While the scoreline reflected a get-right performance, it was a bit underwhelming from La Roja. Don't get me wrong — they played much better than they did against Cabo Verde. But this never felt like a game that could have been 6–0 or 7–0. Spain ended up with an xG of 2.68, meaning that most times this game plays out they score between two and three goals. If you watched how the finishes played out, it felt a little lucky to get four. To be fair to Spain, Saudi Arabia had an xG of 0.08, which means they were never realistically scoring.

Lamine Yamal was able to start today as he eases back from injury and made an instant impact, scoring in the 10th minute at the back post. They just look much more cohesive and dangerous with him on the field. We also talked about the impact Dani Olmo can have, and he also started today, assisting on the second goal — one of the two more repeatable ones of the bunch. I expect we will see both players in the starting lineup moving forward. Overall, Spain put themselves in a strong spot to win the group and move into their last game against Uruguay with a bit more ease. For Saudi Arabia, it is looking… OK. If before the tournament you told me they would earn one point in the first two games and have Cabo Verde in game three, I would say they had put themselves in a position to advance. But after watching two games from each team, I don't think Saudi Arabia is better than Cabo Verde right now. It sets up a very interesting final day where all four teams can still finish in all four positions — though Spain do seem locked into a knockout round spot.

## Belgium vs Iran

This game could have, and probably should have, just been an email. Belgium and Iran fought to a 0–0 draw. First, it must be said that the summer of goalkeepers continued here, as both were incredible and we saw the save of the tournament from Alireza Beiranvand.

There were a few key moments outside of the saves. First, the offside call on an Iranian set-piece play that led to a goal — Taremi was correctly called for being offside and he knows better; this was a planned play with no excuse. They will need to limit these mistakes against Egypt if they want to move on. Then we had what I'm not even sure how to classify — an egregious red card offense that wasn't even given a yellow, as the defender dragged his studs all the way down Trossard's calf, ripping his sock and leaving a nice bruise for the Louvre. About 15 minutes later, Taremi drew a red card on the Belgian defense for denying a clear goal-scoring opportunity. It's a red card for sure, but he went down extremely easily.

So Iran had about 25 minutes to try and score a man up and… it was hard to tell which team was actually up a player. Both teams had chances of roughly equal quality that they failed to convert. In that way, the story of the tournament continued. For Belgium, they have put themselves in an awkward spot, needing a win in their last game to advance. Luckily they play the lowest-ranked team in the group in New Zealand — not that the Kiwis have looked that bad. Belgium's best player, Jeremy Doku, was out today with an illness, so they get a semi-pass. But he and his wife are also expecting their first child in early July, so if Belgium wants to go deep, they may have to do it without him. For Iran, this was supremely disappointing. Up a man for 25 minutes and couldn't muster anything. It does not instill confidence for what comes next, and if they somehow advance, they look primed to get obliterated in the round of 32. They need to be far more aggressive and decisive moving forward.

## Uruguay vs Cabo Verde

Now this is what the tournament is about. The atmosphere in Miami was absolutely electric as Cabo Verde took on Uruguay and earned another draw against another giant of the game. The 2–2 was one of the most entertaining games of the tournament as the massive underdog picked up a second point. Before we crown them champions though, both Cabo Verde goals came from punished mistakes and were a bit fluky. The first was a shot through the wall on a free kick from 30 yards — no way this was the plan, but it worked — and the second was a horrific back pass from Uruguay combined with the goalkeeper coming out when he definitely should not have. That said, they took advantage of mistakes, which is more than most teams have been able to do in this tournament.

For Uruguay, they got both goals in a 10-minute window at the end of the first half and actually played pretty well. It did not look like they took Cabo Verde for granted. However, when you are a top-15 team in the world, you need to be winning this game comfortably. Even at 2–0, it never looked comfortable. Uruguay has drawn both of their games and now has to play Spain in the final round, while Cabo Verde gets Saudi Arabia. They are a bit up a creek here — a draw could see them through, but it requires a very specific set of scenarios.

A brief word on Cabo Verde and how they have gotten to this point. First, let's give them their flowers: first World Cup, first goal, first points, and they have only played countries that have won the World Cup (and there are only eight of them). What have they done well? Their defense has been excellent — as a unit they move very cohesively and the chemistry is off the charts. Vozinha was unbelievable in game one and okay in game two. Their wingers had exceptional games today, and their first-touch turns have consistently put them in space with ample time to play the ball forward. The coaching staff deserves massive credit too — an exceptional plan executed wonderfully in both games, with early and precise substitutions that have had major impacts.

What have they not done well? Two things. First, defending in small groups. When pulled out of their shape or defending in transition, they have been exposed and look significantly less comfortable — though most teams share this weakness. Second, and on full display today, is their quality in the final third. They get there regularly on the counter, but they have not been able to find the last pass, touch, or shot to capitalize on high-leverage chances. This must improve if they are to make the knockout rounds.

## Egypt vs New Zealand

The final game of the day saw us meander up to Vancouver as the Kiwis took on the Pharaohs in what ended up being a 3–1 win for Egypt. New Zealand got out to an early lead and looked extremely competitive, with a real possibility of winning the game through the first half. Egypt didn't look bad, but they weren't looking great either. Then some big halftime adjustments were made and Egypt outclassed the All Whites for the rest of the game — two goals in less than ten minutes, including a nice move finished off by captain Mo Salah, then a third about 15 minutes later. They should have added a fourth in stoppage time, but there was an inventive save from the Kiwis' keeper.

For Egypt, they are firmly in the driver's seat in the group. They move into the final day against a struggling Iran team, and earning just a point is likely enough to advance. For New Zealand, the fairy tale is over. We have seen three great halves of football, but this fourth one looked really bad — almost like they were trying to prove they deserved their lowest-ranking label in the tournament. They also have the strongest team in the group left to play in Belgium, who have the talent to turn things around on a dime. Anything is possible, but I am slowly losing my belief in the Ferns. On the other side, Egypt looked flat in the first half and much more dynamic in the second. A good but not great performance, and they will need to build off of it to move deep in this tournament.
