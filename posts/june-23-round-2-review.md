## Group Stage Round 2 Review

Groups I and J wrapped up their second-round action, and while a couple of results went exactly as expected, we also got a genuine thriller in New York and one of the more forgettable wins of the tournament in San Francisco.

## Argentina vs Austria

Argentina and Austria opened up the account in Dallas. Argentina more or less cruised to a 2–0 win, securing their spot in the knockout stage. People have been talking about the weak groups for all three host nations, and no doubt they are weak — but this one is up there with the weakest. Argentina are going to win this group without really being tested, and I have my doubts about this team. They have scored five times in the tournament, all by Messi. They have looked okay across the rest of the field, showing the occasional bit of skill or creative play, but it looks hard for them to chain these together. This team will live and die with Messi, and they will not have a true test until the round of 16 in all likelihood. I'm also not putting any stock into their last group game, no matter the result — they won't be taking it seriously.

For Austria, this is the expected result, so they are right where they thought they would be after two games. Sabitzer, Alaba, and Laimer have all been underwhelming through two games. All three are going to need to show up in a big way to advance to the knockouts. This Algeria team is good, with players all over the top leagues in Europe and a young, confident squad. They play with a fun arrogance that can lead to them playing above their skill level. Like yesterday's Belgium–Iran game, let's just make this an email next time.

## France vs Iraq

Amidst a downpour in Philly, France and Iraq went about how we expected — an easy 3–0 for Les Bleus. Mbappé got two, moving his tournament total to four, and Dembélé scored his first-ever World Cup goal. This was never really a contest. Iraq had "chances," but I don't think Maignan was ever really worried, nor were the French fans. Because of a rain delay, this game ended only about 45 minutes before the Norway–Senegal game, and spoiler alert — both France and Norway are through.

The French attack looked excellent. They were creating chance after chance in the first 20 minutes and had plenty of opportunities throughout. A 3–0 scoreline honestly undersells how well they played — creative, aggressive, and decisive in attack. This is the France we want to see throughout the World Cup and the France we expect to see against sides like Iraq.

For Iraq, they made a goalkeeper switch after mistakes in the previous game and there was an egregious one today as well. Look, when the tournament started, people flagged this group as containing three trophy contenders and then Iraq. They had the unfortunate pleasure of being in a group with a team that did not lose in qualifying, the prospective African champions, and France. Not a group you want. They will attempt to get their first result in their second World Cup against Senegal, but it seems incredibly unlikely. It was the expected result, and if anything, kind to the Iraqis.

## Senegal vs Norway

New Yorkers really have it best. Their first three games have been Brazil–Morocco, France–Senegal, and now this — a 3–2 thriller until the end. The Norwegians were able to prevail, but there were some nervy moments at the end after Sarr was able to pull one back for the second time for the Lions. On a different day, maybe Senegal win here, and you have to say they were a bit unlucky to face these teams back-to-back. At the same time, they created their own misfortune with big goalkeeping mistakes — one of which led to a goal, and another that should have. You cannot give Haaland any more chances than he earns. He is absolutely deadly in the box and probably the best pure finisher in the tournament. He got a mulligan today, missing out on 0.95 xG — it won't happen again.

With the win, Norway advance to the knockouts. There is a huge difference between first and second place, and because of goal differential, France have the draw odds — so Norway's job is not done yet. For one of the cooler scenes in this World Cup, watch captain Martin Ødegaard lead the Norwegian fans in their rowdy cheer after the final whistle. An awesome moment.

Jumping back to Senegal, they have looked a bit disjointed. Goalkeeper Édouard Mendy can be all over the place — at his best he reaches the highs of any goalkeeper in the world, but that has definitely not been on display here. The rest of the team has been hit-or-miss, with both Mané and Sarr having spurts but never together. The holding midfielders, who were supposed to be a strength, have not controlled the game as much as needed, and while they haven't been bad, I wouldn't call them a strength either. One bright spot: 18-year-old Ibrahim Mbaye has looked excellent in both appearances off the bench. He adds another dimension down the side and in the middle of the field. He is currently at PSG, so it's not like we are hiding this guy from the world — let him stretch his legs for the full 90 against Iraq. If I understand correctly, Senegal can still advance and will need to come out scoring in that final game.

## Jordan vs Algeria

The final game of the day saw the Fennecs face off against Al-Nashama in San Francisco, one of the more bizarre games of the tournament. Algeria beat Jordan 2–1 in a result that felt wrong for most of the match. In their first game, Jordan looked really competitive — pressing, keeping possession, getting chances, like playing ping-pong with a second player and waiting for the varying response from Austria. But tonight against Algeria, it felt like they were playing ping-pong against a wall. A one-player game. They were hustling, their effort was sky-high, and they defended relatively well — but it was just so, so passive. They had 13% less possession, giving them a near-tournament low at 25%. A massive regression and a big missed opportunity to get their first points in their first World Cup. They were able to score first but could not hold on in the second half, giving up two goals from corners — and arguably both were goalkeeper mistakes. The first one definitely was; the second is more debatable.

Losing 2–1 like this stings, and they now face Argentina, which is going to be a learning experience with effectively no chance of a result. For Algeria, this was a rough game to watch. You are playing the weakest team in your group, you have a plethora of talent on the field, and you cannot score from open play — cannot even really threaten from open play. They were two goalkeeper errors away from losing this game. The difference between Algeria and Türkiye is that one team played better goalies. Disappointing even in victory. I feel like neither team deserved to win, but if anyone did, it was the Jordanians. That said, Algeria get the three points and now move into a matchup against Austria for second place in the group. Austria has looked equally uninspiring so far. This game is shaping up to be either an incredible back-and-forth epic — or two idiots hitting each other with limp pool noodles. My guess is the latter. A draw sees both teams through, with Austria getting second and Algeria third on goal differential. Overall, one of the more forgettable matches of the tournament.
