## Phase 3 Preview — Groups A, B & C

Six games tomorrow as the first day of simultaneous play starts. Instead of looking at each game individually, I'm going to lay out the state of each group, share a few notes, and give my predictions. For a live look at how the third-place standings are shaping up, the BBC has an excellent page with all the information you need: [BBC World Cup Schedule](https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/world-cup/schedule).

## Group B

Group B starts us off as Canada and Switzerland square off in Toronto, and Bosnia and Qatar face off in Seattle. The first match is playing for top spot; the second is playing for third.

Canada have draw odds here, but expect the Swiss to come out swinging — they will want to show they are clearly the best team in the group and take first place. Canada have built up a massive goal differential and would have to lose by four or five goals to miss out on second place even in a loss. I want to see more of Manzambi for the Swiss and expect them to get a hard-earned but overall comfortable win.

**Prediction: Switzerland 2–0 Canada**

Both Bosnia and Qatar need wins to advance, so expect them to come way out of the defensive shells both teams have been in so far. For me, Bosnia has clearly been the better team in the tournament and has much more talent. I'm looking for Džeko to get involved early and for the young Bosnian midfielders to outplay the Qatari middle. Expecting a bit of a thumping here.

**Prediction: Bosnia 4–0 Qatar**

*Note: For all other potential third-place teams, you are rooting for a draw here so that both of these teams are eliminated — which would be an extremely apropos result for how this group has gone.*

## Group C

Morocco and Haiti meet in Atlanta, while the Brazilians and Scots have been taking over Miami for the last few days.

Haiti have been eliminated, so they are playing spoiler here — but I don't think they have the talent to challenge Morocco. The Atlas Lions are going to try to pour it on because they want first place and are currently down on goal differential to Brazil. I'm looking for a big game from Brahim Díaz to create more chances than he has in the previous two games. Morocco will need him, and all of those lightbulbs are going to get a workout on the scoreboard. Expecting a big game from Morocco — but not quite big enough.

**Prediction: Morocco 3–0 Haiti**

For Brazil, the lineup change seemed to make a big difference as Cunha looked much more comfortable in the squad than Thiago. I expect to see the same lineup against the Tartan Army. The Scots have had about 20 minutes of total time looking reasonable in this tournament, sandwiched by a lot of lackluster play. They will likely need something out of this game to advance to the knockouts. Ultimately, I think we get the best game of the day here — but not quite enough for Scotland. Brazil edges it out with a late goal to seal it as the Scots press.

**Prediction: Brazil 3–1 Scotland**

## Group A

Mexico have wrapped up the group as they take on Czechia in Mexico City, and South Korea complete their Mexican tour in Monterrey against South Africa.

Not sure what to expect from Mexico here, other than that I think Guillermo Ochoa gets the start in what will likely be his last appearance in the Mexican international jersey. It's a fitting send-off for a legend in the stadium he has owned for many years. This Czech team hasn't shown me anything so far — to me, they have been a bottom-five team in the tournament. I'm not expecting much. Mexico will continue to look mediocre, but they aren't losing Ochoa's last game.

**Prediction: Mexico 1–0 Czechia**

One of the only teams that has looked worse than Czechia is South Africa. I expect South Korea to walk all over them. This golf-style, two-touches-from-goalkeeper-to-shot play is not going to work against any team — but especially not one as well-organized as South Korea.

**Prediction: South Korea 3–0 South Africa**
