## Group Stage Round 2 Review

Phase 2 of the group stage is in the books. We got one blowout, one brutally physical nil-nil, a hard-fought 1–0, and a forgettable closer in Guadalajara. Then we'll do a full Phase 2 recap before looking ahead.

## Portugal vs Uzbekistan

Houston, we have a problem. The White Wolves lost a couple of pack mates in a 5–0 defeat to the Portuguese. In a five-goal win, everyone is going to look like they played well, but I'd like to call out a couple of players specifically.

First, Ronaldo. He got his two goals and easily could have had a third or more. Excellent for his confidence and the team's — but I am not sure this is completely unexpected against one of the lowest-rated teams in the tournament. He looked good today, and more importantly than his finishing, he was back to his active self — putting in effort on both sides of the ball and chasing and pressing into the 90th minute. He needs to do this to be at his best.

That said, this team moves through Bruno Fernandes and the players on the outside — notably Nuno Mendes and Nélson Semedo. They have a plethora of speed on the edge, which allows their high-skill midfielders to control the game relatively easily. If this team is going to reach or exceed expectations, it will come through the midfield, not through Ronaldo.

For Uzbekistan, this is about the exposure the game gets for the country. The president — he's a dictator — has changed the scheduling of their game days so the country can watch the matches. There are watch parties and activities set up around the world, so even in a 5–0 loss, the country will adore the players upon their return. This was a game where they hardly threatened — even though they had a goal taken off the board from a spectacular hit, the result was never in danger for Portugal. Uzbekistan needs to regroup and head into their final matchday looking to get a point. The DRC is the worst team they will have played, and they can certainly fight for a draw or even a win. That game on Sunday is their World Cup final. Get into that mentality and prepare mentally, physically, and emotionally. Go out there like you might never play in the World Cup again.

## England vs Ghana

The Three Lions met the Black Stars in Boston, a city the English historically struggle in. Sort of like 250 years ago, the underdog won via a draw — in this case, 0–0. This was a brutal game physically, exactly what we expect in a colonization battle, and the referee had an awful day. Ghana committed twice as many fouls as England, but both teams ended up with the same number of yellow cards. It felt like the ref had limited control. There was a weird first-half double hydration break due to a head collision where both players stayed in the game and one of them had his head wrapped — is there even a concussion protocol? Then, of course, there was the egregious missed penalty for Ghana, which could have led to a massive upset.

You have to give it up to Ghana — it was horrid to watch, but they had an excellent defensive game plan and executed it nearly flawlessly. There was about a 90-second period where England looked like they might score near the 90th minute, but other than that, they looked completely miffed. The English definitely looked worse than in game one, but it is tough to evaluate them for the knockout rounds because most teams will not play this aggressively on defense. Four points effectively locks both teams into the knockout rounds. The English are looking to win the group and the tournament, and for Ghana, their first goal has been achieved.

## Croatia vs Panama

The checkered jerseys were out in Toronto today as the Croatians squeaked out a 1–0 win against the Panamanians. Today marked Luka Modrić collecting his 200th appearance for the national team — a feat only four people have ever achieved. While he was the worst player on the field against England, he was much better today, though still only average. We have not seen the world-class Luka quite yet.

That said, I want to talk about two players on the Croatian side. First, goalkeeper Dominik Livaković — he was the best player on the field today, and while he wasn't as good as he was in game one, how could he have been? The second is attacking midfielder Martin Baturina. He is only 23 and plays for Como (yes, Lake Como) in Serie A, who are going to the Champions League for the first time ever — if you want to pick up a club, this is a fun story and a fun team to root for. His first touch was on point today, as it usually is, and he was able to control the game and distribute the ball very well. I think we are seeing a bit of a changing of the guard. As Modrić ends his international career, a new star is being born. What is it with Croatia and their midfielders?

For Panama, it was better today. They looked threatening and drew the best out of Livaković, although it felt like he was simply not giving up a goal today. They were just missing that killer instinct in the box and the final third. There is no star on this team — no one for the players to look to in the nervy moments of the match. All of the players are at such a high talent level that part of what separates teams at this stage is the mental side: staying within yourself, not panicking, trusting your mechanics. Unfortunately for our canal friends, they have been eliminated from the 2026 World Cup.

## Congo DR vs Colombia

Our final game of Phase 2 was hosted in Guadalajara, with 50,000 Colombian fans cheering on their boys against Congo DR. Huh — this is our daily game that just could have been an email. Feels like we have been getting one of these per day.

A bit disappointed in the Colombian performance despite the 1–0 win. They looked inspired, creative, and full of ideas for the first 15 minutes, and then it was a lot of meh. They have a relatively skilled lineup but look undisciplined and lacking chemistry — a bit clunky moving around the field at times. They were offside seven times today, which is a ton. There will be teams in the tournament that won't have that many offsides the entire time they are playing. This is the kind of lack of discipline that loses games, and most of them were easily avoidable. If they go into the match against Portugal playing like this, they are going to have a bad time.

It must be said that it is the summer of the goalkeeper, as Lionel Mpasi was excellent. Some of his saves were relatively routine, but others were outrageous, and he really deserved better than the deflected goal that ended up being the difference. For Congo, it's okay — they got one point from their first two games and only have a goal differential of –1. They can still advance with a win against Uzbekistan, the weakest team they will have played so far.

## Phase 2 Recap & Standings

That wraps up the second phase of group stage games. Tomorrow starts Phase 3, where we will have six games a day — two at a time, with groups kicking off simultaneously to prevent cheating, backroom deals, or teams playing to a specific result because they already know what they need. We start off by concluding Groups A, B, and C, and by the end of the weekend, we will know who has advanced to the knockout rounds.

**Teams that have secured their spot in the knockouts:**

- France
- Germany
- United States
- Norway
- Argentina
- Colombia
- Mexico

While Germany, the US, Argentina, and Mexico have secured first place in their groups, I have some caveats. I know Argentina haven't given up a goal yet and have scored five, but they have not looked that great to me. Sure, Messi still has it, but they have played two of the most overrated and underperforming teams in the tournament, and it hasn't looked convincing from anyone but Leo. I also know Mexico has won their group, but they have looked bad in their first two games — lucky to have played one of the worst teams in the tournament and to have been the beneficiaries of the biggest goalkeeping howler of the tournament.

**Teams performing above expectations:**

These are the teams that have looked the best through two games relative to their predicted performance — the dark horses poised to make some noise in the knockouts:

**United States:** Look, it has only been two games, but so far so great for the Stars and Stripes. I would put exactly zero stock into the final group game against Türkiye, as the US is playing for nothing and the Turkish team is either completely morally defeated or going to be playing with a combination of apathy and anger. Anything past the Round of 16 is electric for the US.

**Australia:** Playing a counter-attacking style to catch opponents off guard. Their win against Türkiye looks slightly less impressive now, but I am expecting a win against Paraguay to secure their knockout spot. Any win in the knockouts will be heavily celebrated down under.

**Ivory Coast:** A lot of people were expecting this performance from the Elephants, but I have been pleasantly surprised. They are extremely physical and have players who are very skilled on the ball. While they have not secured their ticket yet, they sit at a neutral goal differential and play Curaçao next — it seems impossible not to advance from this spot. This is a team that can knock off a top contender, as they nearly did against Germany.

**Netherlands:** I know this is a team that is always in contention, but I thought they would be a bit mid-tier this time around. Instead, they might be the team that has looked the best in the tournament so far. Prior to the tournament I would have called them a generous, un-fun dark horse pick. Now I am rating them right up there with the favorites.

**Japan:** They have been a solid team at past World Cups but have looked particularly great here. While they aren't officially through yet, they almost certainly will be given their goal differential on four points. They were able to draw a very strong Dutch team and have a midfield that sets and controls the pace of the game. They have a brutal first knockout matchup regardless of where they finish, but expect them to challenge (likely against Brazil or Morocco).

**Cabo Verde:** Get on the damn bandwagon! Two draws against former World Cup winners and soccer powerhouses. They go against Saudi Arabia next and likely need a win to advance, but a draw could get it done. I would love to see them in the knockouts — the vibes would be immaculate. Let's go, Blue Sharks!

**Ghana:** Saved the biggest surprise for last. This was the lowest-rated of the African sides to make the tournament, and they have looked really strong. They executed their game plan well in both games and are now very likely to advance, even if they lose to Croatia. Their first knockout game would be a brutal one — potentially against Portugal — but if they manage to get Colombia, I could see the Black Stars going back to the Round of 16.
