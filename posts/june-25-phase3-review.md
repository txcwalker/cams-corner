## Phase 3 Review — Groups A, B & C

## Group B

As with the previews, I am going to try to keep these shorter and more digestible with six games a day. Things in Group B went about as expected.

The battle of the reds took place in Vancouver — not Cincinnati — as Switzerland and Canada faced off in one of the more forgettable first halves of the World Cup. We did get to see Manzambi start and he looked good; I can't imagine he won't start again in the Round of 32. A couple of big chances were missed by Switzerland early, meaning this could have been much worse than 2–1. The breakthrough came early in the second half — the first goal was standard, but the second was a pretty gross goalkeeping mistake. Canada's goal came from two moments of really high quality, specifically two excellent first touches. The better team won and is taking first place in the group, which is a deserved result for Switzerland. It looks like Canada will be facing South Africa, which we will get to shortly.

For the other game down the road in Seattle, Bosnia and Qatar battled for a potential third-place spot. Again, it went just about as expected. Both teams hit the post in what ended up being a comfortable 3–1 win for Bosnia. Džeko got his 200th cap and made a pretty decent impact — causing an own goal and hitting the post. But the day belonged to 18-year-old Kerim Alajbegović. He scored an absolute screamer that will be tough to beat for goal of the tournament. I've been harping on these young guys on national teams and the importance of letting them play and strut their stuff — this group gave us two shining examples of young players having massive impacts for their countries. With the win, Bosnia are nearly certain to advance given the results in the other groups.

Lastly, this was a disaster of a World Cup for Qatar. They took a step backward in terms of soccer culture. They played an interesting style but looked completely overmatched on the field — multiple red cards, multiple extremely poor performances. They are extremely fortunate to even have a point, and they will need to reevaluate everything moving forward.

## Group C

Again, things went about as expected. Starting with Brazil and Scotland facing off in Miami.

Brazil pretty easily cruised to a 3–0 win against the Scots. A brace from Vini Jr. and Cunha got the third. The Scots looked like they were playing their opponent rather than the ball, and from a neutral state, they played like they were already losing. A forgettable tournament from Scotland — it seems unlikely they will advance given what happened in Group A.

I do want to take a moment to talk about Brazil. This feels like a train that is very quickly gaining momentum, and if they don't lose their next game, they might not lose at all. We got to see both teenagers in Rayan and Endrick. While neither was spectacular, it's good to get them game time before being thrown into a knockout situation. Neymar made his first appearance, looked fine, and I assume he will come off the bench when the situation calls for it.

But the real story is that they seem to have figured out the midfield. Casemiro being a beast at the back was never an issue — he has been world-class his whole career, winning the ball back and making tough challenges. Bruno Guimarães has been exactly what you want from a box-to-box midfielder: in two places at once, doing both things well. The missing pieces have been Lucas Paquetá settling into his number 10 role and Matheus Cunha successfully attracting attention as the striker and finding space. This team is always a fun, creative, and enterprising team to watch on offense. As long as the back four hold up, this will be a tough out for anyone in the tournament.

Just up the road in Atlanta, Morocco and Haiti delivered one of the best halves of soccer of the tournament — the first half ended 2–2. Ultimately the Atlas Lions were too much for the Haitians, scoring twice in the second half for a 4–2 win.

First, Haiti's second goal — scored by Wilson Isidor — was an absolute rocket, a worldie. Beautifully placed with about as much power as you can get behind a strike. Other than that, Haiti played above their talent level but were still outclassed. Honestly, they had a competitive tournament and can hold their heads high — zero points, but they were in both games for the full 90 minutes. Looking forward to what they can do in the future, hopefully in a country that will have better sporting infrastructure soon.

For Morocco, I know they scored four times, but this was a bit disappointing. A few very easy chances were missed. They moved the ball well, but the recurring theme of this tournament was on full display: lack of ability to finish in the box. They will need to clean this up heading into a likely matchup with the Dutch. One last note — their star players continue to get subbed out toward the end of games, and this worries me a bit. Most teams that are advancing deep have had their main guys playing 90+ minutes at game speed. I am a little worried about what that means for Morocco moving forward.

## Group A

The final group of the day saw two games in Mexico — Mexico and Czechia in Mexico City, and South Korea against South Africa in Monterrey.

First of all, congratulations to South Africa on their first-ever knockout stage appearance. This is a big deal, and I look forward to seeing their soccer culture continue to develop because of it.

But South Korea — what happened? They played like South Africa did in their game against Mexico. A complete reversal of trajectory: South Korea got worse and worse as the tournament went on, while South Africa got better and better. The first rule of soccer is that when you pass the ball, you move, and if you don't have the ball, you move to get it. Tonight I saw a lot of statues on the field. Embarrassing. It is really hard to pass to an open man or break down a defense when your targets are standing still. This has been one of the themes of the tournament for me, alongside the lack of finishing and goalkeeping masterpieces — a lot of stagnant offensive play. With this result, South Korea are likely out, and it must be said, deservedly so.

For Bafana Bafana, what a turnaround. They have still looked awful in all three games, but it was a better version of awful against South Korea. They are going to need to figure out a lot before Sunday, but hey — all they care about right now is that they made it through.

As for Mexico–Czechia — a snoozefest. Mexico got the 3–0 win, and Czechia will be exiting with a deserved last place in the group. I'm not really going to talk about the game itself other than to say Mexico again took advantage of a weak group and teams that were not up to the challenge. They probably looked their best here, but I am still highly skeptical of this team moving forward.

What I do want to talk about is Guillermo Ochoa getting subbed on late. This was an awesome moment to witness and something the manager absolutely got right. Seeing him jog out onto the field and receive a standing ovation at Estadio Azteca was a thing of beauty. He also had a nice moment with his family on the field at the end. For the uninitiated — he has played in six World Cups and is 40 years old. For many teams and generations, he defined the Mexican national team as their headline player, a consistent force through the years. I can't tell you how many US–Mexico games I watched thinking the US might win, only to see their chances squashed by a sprawling Ochoa. This is a bit like seeing Derek Jeter's final game at Yankee Stadium, Tim Duncan in San Antonio, or Larry Fitzgerald's last game with the Cardinals. Ochoa wasn't playing in the same domestic leagues, but he was playing in the world's biggest sporting event. He is every bit the legend in Mexican soccer that they are in their respective sports.

At the end of all the games today, we know our first locked-in knockout matchup — and everyone, put your tinfoil hats on. Los Angeles will host the first game of the knockout stage, and the only game on Sunday the 28th will be Canada vs South Africa. A host country getting the second-place finisher and possibly the weakest team to advance in the tournament, with no other games on the schedule. Hmmmmmmmmmm. Both teams will think they can win, and both are in their first-ever World Cup knockout stage. We will have a first-time winner.
