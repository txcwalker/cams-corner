## Phase 3 Preview — Groups D, E & F

## Group E

Group E starts us off on Day 2 of simultaneous play, as Curaçao and Ivory Coast meet in Philly, and Ecuador faces Germany in New York.

For the Ivorians, they get their easiest group game last. Since they won against Ecuador, a draw is likely good enough — but they aren't going to want to leave that to chance, especially against one of the weakest teams in the tournament. They can't finish first and are basically locked into second, but they can still technically finish last if things go disastrously wrong. I'm looking for Yann Diomandé to continue his excellent form and for the team to shake off a difficult, last-second loss that was plagued by some brutal mistakes.

**Prediction: Ivory Coast 3–0 Curaçao**

Germany and Ecuador is an interesting matchup. While Germany prefers a more free-flowing style, they are never ones to shy away from physical play. Since Germany have locked up first place, expect to see mostly reserves here, but they are still playing to win — tournament integrity remains intact. For evaluating Germany's true strength, this game will be mostly useless. Can their B-team beat an Ecuador side playing for their World Cup lives? Probably. For me, Ecuador have not shown enough in their first two games. Moisés Caicedo has been great, but the rest of the team is not executing at the level you would expect given their club-level talent and form. If they can find that form, they have a chance — but they'll have to play more attacking than they normally would. I see the Germans scoring late against a pressing Ecuador.

**Prediction: Germany 2–1 Ecuador**

## Group F

Group F takes center stage as the second group to finish up. Tunisia takes on the Netherlands in Kansas City, and Japan goes back to Dallas to meet Sweden.

Starting with Kansas City — this is going to be brutal. We have watched the Eagles of Carthage get dismantled twice now, 5–1 and 4–0. Now they face their most formidable opponent, who need to win by as much as possible since they are competing for the top spot and neither team has draw odds against Japan. Expecting another bloodbath.

**Prediction: Netherlands 5–0 Tunisia (or more)**

Sweden and Japan is the most interesting game of the day — both teams can technically still finish first or third in the group. Japan, at four points and +4 goal differential, are likely through no matter what, so the game is a bit more important for Sweden. Despite coming off a 5–1 loss, the Swedes have looked good in both games and I am expecting them to compete at a high level. Again, it will come down to whether Sweden can score enough to cover their defensive weaknesses. In normal circumstances I would say this likely ends in a draw, but I don't think the Swedish backline can hold. I like Japan to win, with Sweden grabbing one late to make the last 15 minutes interesting — but failing to fully break through.

**Prediction: Japan 3–2 Sweden**

## Group D

The hosts take the prime-time limelight to end the night, as the US and Türkiye meet in Los Angeles, while Paraguay and Australia play just up the I-5 in San Francisco.

Even as an American, I can't get too excited for the US–Türkiye game. Türkiye are eliminated and locked into last, and the US are locked into first. I expect experimental lineups from both teams for rest and experience reasons. Zero stock in this game. I'm guessing we don't see Christian Pulisic here — even though it would be nice to get him some game-speed reps, it's not worth the risk of re-aggravating his injury.

For the game that actually matters — Paraguay and Australia in the battle for second place. The Socceroos have draw odds here, so look for them to play in their defensive, counter-attacking style. Four points likely sees both teams through, but the situation is much more dangerous for Paraguay, who would be the third-place team on a –2 goal differential with only one goal scored in that scenario. Both teams will have the results of the earlier groups before kickoff, so they will know exactly what happened to the other third-place teams. It's possible — though unlikely — that they will know a draw locks in both teams. Missing Miguel Almirón, and given what Paraguay have shown so far, I am expecting another Australian masterclass, likely eliminating Paraguay.

**Prediction: Australia 2–0 Paraguay**
