## Phase 3 Review — Groups G, H & I

## Group I

## Norway vs France

As I alluded to, Norway benched most of their starters and played the second team today. They were at minimum getting 2nd, and were locked into playing Côte d'Ivoire if that were to be the case. So they started the B team, and it went about as you would expect. France ends up with a 4–1 win thanks to Golden Ball winner Ousmane Dembélé. France was in rhythm for the first half and happy to sit back and defend. Norway missed some golden opportunities in the box that Haaland and company usually finish off with no problem (this included a missed penalty). It's tough to truly evaluate either team. I will say that the Norwegian backup goalie had an excellent day despite giving up 4 goals. Not much else to say based on Norway benching their team. For the record, I do think it was the correct decision; players will be fresh for a game they were already going to have to play anyway.

## Senegal vs Iraq

This again went as predicted, even by me, I believe, getting the exact scoreline of 5–0. Senegal scored early and then were able to draw a red card shortly after. It was 1–0 going into halftime, and then Iraq eventually was chasing the game too much and the goals came pouring in for Senegal. They have played their way into being almost certainly the best team on 3 points, and I believe, after the results from the day, are guaranteed to be advancing. Should be a very interesting game if they do in fact end up playing England in the Round of 32.

For Iraq, what can you say? They were put in the hardest group of the tournament. Numerically they end up having the worst tournament of any team, but they definitely passed the eye test more than Tunisia did. Despite the ugly exit and performance, they did make it to the World Cup, and for a team and country that does not have the best soccer culture, they should use this as a building block moving forward.

## Group H

## Cape Verde Islands vs Saudi Arabia

No sense in burying the lead here: congratulations to the Blue Sharks as they drew their third game in a row (0–0 here) and end up finishing second in the group. They become the first team since 1998 to advance in second place without winning a game. For the country and the culture, this is an absolutely electric moment. They played well enough to win and ended up missing a golden opportunity at the end of the game. Can't say enough good things here. What a time to be Cabo Verdean; everyone get on the bandwagon, here we go. Argentina who? Messi who? Blue Sharks to the final.

For Saudi Arabia, it was sort of an as-expected tournament. They looked okay against Uruguay, poor against Spain, and just sad against Cabo Verde. They were not playing with any urgency for pretty much the entire game, even though they knew they needed a win. All that being said, they had a golden opportunity to win the game at the end, and the shot went straight to Vozinha in goal. They are hosting in 8 years, so they are running out of time to get to a spot where they can compete when they host. Overall, still expect to see them in four years; it wasn't a pathetic showing like we saw from Iraq or Tunisia.

## Spain vs Uruguay

Well, the meltdown is complete for Uruguay. They lose 1–0 thanks to a complete howler — the worst mistake of the tournament — from their most accomplished goalkeeper, Fernando Muslera. He made several mistakes leading to goals and dropped points this tournament. You hate to point the finger at a single individual for not qualifying for the knockouts, but yikes. He even came out of retirement for this tournament. Woof. He was benched for the second half of the game and was not seen on the field after halftime. It's a brutal way to leave the national team, but not everyone gets a fairytale ending.

However, his exit wasn't even the worst of the day for the team. Agustín Canobbio was aggressive in the worst ways. The ref gave him way too much leash — he should have had several yellow cards before this point — and then in 90+3, he had what will end up being the most egregious red card tackle of the tournament. This is the kind of thing that breaks a leg. He is lucky that the Spanish players didn't jump and curb-stomp him afterward. After the ref promptly gave him the card, he was in his face screaming and had to be pulled back by his own teammates. He continued to display awful behavior and sportsmanship by berating the 4th official, the Spanish bench, and their coaching staff. We will almost certainly never see him at a World Cup again, nor in a Uruguay national jersey. I wouldn't be shocked if we saw FIFA/UEFA step in and give him a ban at the club level. This kind of stuff is unacceptable, especially on the world's biggest stage.

For Spain, it felt like they were coasting a bit here. They end up topping the group. The manager was experimenting a bit with the lineup, getting some players on the field who are unlikely to see it in the knockouts. They sort of just put on the bomb squad suits and watched Uruguay implode on themselves.

## Group G

## Belgium vs New Zealand

Well, Belgium are back… maybe. They notched a nice 5–1 win over the Kiwis, and we finally saw why this team came in as the lowest-ranked in the tournament. Belgium did exactly what you would want a top-10 team to do against a bottom team and completely smashed them. New Zealand hardly saw the ball the whole game. They looked completely overmatched the entire game, though they did have one nice moment of quality by striker Elijah Just, who gets his third goal of the tournament. For Belgium, they created space, were creative in and around the box, and while they missed some chances, they also had some great finishing on display. Time will tell if that was New Zealand being bad or Belgium being good.

## Egypt vs Iran

Okay, let's just get this out of the way for how offsides works: you must be behind at least two defenders, which can include the goalkeeper. So, on Iran's late goal that was taken off the board, it was in fact called correctly. Because the goalkeeper came out to get the ball and was in front of the ball and the offensive player when the initial ball was played in, offsides was correctly called.

The rest of this game was pretty much a snoozefest. The game finishes 1–1, which sees Belgium in 1st, Egypt in 2nd (on goal differential), and Iran in 3rd and likely to advance. Both teams scored kind of junk goals and really didn't have anything else to give. Interestingly for Egypt, they did not start Omar Marmoush, and instead, he came off the bench to start the second half. Egypt is now locked into their knockout match against Australia. Neither team looked impressive here, so it should be interesting to see how they fare in the knockouts.
