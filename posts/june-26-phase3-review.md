## Phase 3 Review — Groups D, E & F

## Group E

## Ivory Coast vs Curaçao

The first group of the day was Group E as Germany tried to knock out Ecuador in New York and Ivory Coast looked to sign the dotted line against Curaçao in Philadelphia. The Ivorians looked good, as they should have — but it is tough to take too much from this game. Curaçao was one of the weakest teams in the tournament by ranking, and they looked it. That said, they managed their first point and first goal. It's a good start to grow the soccer culture on the island, though there is a long way to go before it gets to baseball level. Worth a reminder that 25 of their 26 players were born in the Netherlands. For the Elephants, congratulations are in order as they make their first knockout round, and they earned it too. It looks like they will be facing Norway, which is a decent matchup for them. So far, we haven't seen a team really have an answer to Yann Diomandé down the side of the field.

## Germany vs Ecuador

Germany vs Ecuador was a wild one, with two goals in the first ten minutes — one for each side. Ecuador found a late winner and held on to win 2–1. I believe they are guaranteed through as one of the best third-place teams. Moisés Caicedo finally got some of the help he needed from his teammates — he looked excellent again today and had his fingerprints all over this game. When he is not the only player on the field for Ecuador, this team can beat most in the competition. While Germany were already locked into first, I think they might be trending slightly in the wrong direction. In typical German fashion they played their starting XI and were really unable to capitalize on the chances they created. This is a different conversation if some of those go in, but they didn't. It looks like they will have Paraguay in the first stage of the knockouts, which would be a blessing for them — but things are still subject to change.

## Group F

Next up was Group F, one of the more exciting groups in the tournament. Japan and Sweden squared off in Dallas, and the Dutch handled Tunisia in KC.

## Netherlands vs Tunisia

Let's start in KC. The Dutch had two goals inside ten minutes and it never looked uncomfortable for them, controlling the game all the way through in a 3–1 win — only giving up a goal on a set piece, which is one area Tunisia is competent in. There isn't a ton to talk about here. The Dutch looked really good again and deservedly top the group. For their troubles they get Morocco — better than Brazil, I suppose. We will talk more on the Dutch in a couple of days, but they look like a strong favorite. Tunisia will rather ungracefully exit the World Cup. Through three games, this was the worst showing by a long shot from any team — two own goals, never looked competitive, unoriginal, and fired their coach after game one (which, to be fair, is pretty original). They did not play an entertaining brand of soccer. This is a massive step back from 2022, and they will need at least one cycle, maybe two, to regroup and fix it. It starts with finding the right coach and giving him plenty of time to implement a system and culture.

## Japan vs Sweden

In Dallas we had a pretty exciting 1–1 draw between two good teams. Japan played better and definitely had more chances, but it's tough to say Sweden didn't earn their point. Anthony Elanga scored again and was the brightest spot for Sweden — their whole front line looked good. I know he had a tough season at Nottingham Forest in the Premier League, but he is showing up wearing his flag. His goal looks a little awkward because of the keeper's positioning — just know everyone is doing everything correctly here: perfect ball in, perfect placement, and the keeper must try to react while staying home for a potential header. For Japan, this is an exciting team to watch. They play a very technical and fun brand of soccer. It is disappointing that a few of their best players are out with injuries, but I'm really liking the growth, style, and culture I am seeing from this team. For their trouble of finishing second in the group, they get to face Brazil.

## Group D

The final Group D matches took center stage as Türkiye and the US faced off in LA in a completely meaningless game, while just up the I-5, Australia and Paraguay were scheduled to fight for second place.

## United States vs Turkey

Türkiye ended up winning 3–2 in the 98th minute. It's tough to take anything away from this game as nearly all the reserves were in, with only a couple of exceptions. They looked okay — about how you'd expect guys without much history together to look. Two positives worth noting. First, Sebastian Berhalter was excellent tonight, probably Man of the Match. He does get subbed in during most games, so good to see him in the spotlight — comfortable and confident on the ball, really controlling the game in the center. Second, we did see Pulisic early in the second half, and man, this guy is good. He completely changed the game and made an instant impact. The US is much better and a completely different animal with him on the field.

For Türkiye, we saw the talent on display. I am not going to call them unlucky — a good team should be able to beat a defensive shell no matter how organized. But they had to face the one type of team they hadn't worked out how to beat, and they had to do it twice. They need one moment of quality from Hakan Çalhanoğlu or Arda Güler. We did see it tonight from Güler in both the goal and the piece of skill to beat two defenders in the 90th minute. Expect the Turks to be back in 2030 with more young, high-skilled players and the core back with experience and understanding of how to play in tournaments.

## Australia vs Paraguay

The email of the day — Australia and Paraguay drew 0–0. For Australia, that is fine; they knew it was all they needed since they had draw odds for second place. Neither team looked particularly exciting, but Australia was significantly better. For Paraguay, who were not guaranteed through with a draw, this was a cowardly display — completely pathetic. The fact that they are advancing over Türkiye is a complete scam. They had a chance to significantly improve their round of 32 matchup and try to win the game, but they decided to play for a draw. Disgusting. I think I counted one touch, maybe two, in the Australian 18-yard box for Paraguay. I am not sure most of the players knew they were allowed to go into that box, let alone score. They are almost locked into a date with Germany, and one can only hope they get obliterated if they are going to continue to play like this.
