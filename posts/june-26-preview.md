## Phase 3 Preview — Groups G, H & I

## Group I

## Norway vs France

The penultimate day of group stage action kicks off with Group I as Norway and France decide the winner of the group, and Senegal desperately fights for the knockout stage against Iraq. Boston gets to host a potential game of the group stage as the Row, Row, Row Your Boat team tries to pull off the upset against Les Bleus. Look, we all want to see France lose just for some balance in the world, but I just can't see it here. They have an advantage at every position that isn't striker — where Haaland is maybe slightly better than Mbappé, though it's probably closer to a push. France looks like world champions in a 3–1 win.

**Prediction: France 3–1 Norway**

## Senegal vs Iraq

This Senegal team might be the best team to not have a point after their first two games in the World Cup. Luckily for them, eight of the best third-place teams advance. It is possible it will be mathematically impossible for them to advance by the end of today's games, but they won't know that yet — so I expect them to put on a show against a bottom-five tournament side in Iraq. Expect to see PSG youngster Ibrahim Mbaye, as he has been a bright spot off the bench in both games. Iraq has given up seven goals and had major goalkeeping and defensive mistakes in both games, so expect another free goal or two here. Shield your eyes in Toronto — this one is going to get ugly as Senegal tries to recover some goal differential to end up as one of, at best, two teams on three points to advance.

**Prediction: Senegal 5–0 Iraq**

## Group H

## Cape Verde Islands vs Saudi Arabia

Next up is Group H as Cabo Verde goes back to Houston to battle Saudi Arabia, and Uruguay meets a recovered Spain in Guadalajara — where three dialects of Spanish meet and guarantee no one can understand each other. If you aren't on the Cabo Verde bandwagon, I am not sure what you are doing here. This has been the story of the World Cup so far, and pretty much every country not named Saudi Arabia will be rooting for the Blue Sharks. The public will be behind them, but I don't think it is so open-and-shut. Of course this is their easiest game, but it will be a relatively even matchup, and I still expect Cabo Verde to play their counter-attacking style. Interestingly, it is possible a draw is good enough for second place depending on whether Spain wins — which is why we have simultaneous games. They will be playing to win, but you better believe if Spain goes up two goals, Cabo Verde will be parking the bus. This is the first game where I see all three results as legitimately possible. I'll say 1–0 Cabo Verde, as Saudi Arabia gets impatient too early and Cabo Verde is able to score on the counter. That might be wishful thinking — in an even game, everything is on the table.

**Prediction: Cabo Verde 1–0 Saudi Arabia**

## Spain vs Uruguay

For Spain, we saw them recover a bit of form in game two — but it still looked a bit shaky. It must be said that after Lamine Yamal came out at halftime, they looked much worse and less threatening, only managing to get an own goal to push their lead to 4–0. This Uruguay team has underperformed significantly so far — even more than Spain — and they should be on six points if it weren't for some pretty egregious errors in both games. While the team is great, I expect Spain to strut their stuff here and show why many thought they were tournament favorites. They will hold the ball for 70%+ of the time and slowly constrict Uruguay out of the game. If Uruguay has a chance, it will come when Lamine Yamal is off the field and they can take advantage of the few chances they get in that window.

**Prediction: Spain 2–0 Uruguay**

## Group G

## Egypt vs Iran

For Egypt and Iran, this is an interesting game. Egypt has been solid in their first two games, with their talent up top being the main reason they are on four points. For Iran, it feels like their games have been up-and-down the entire time. Most concerningly, they were up a player for nearly 30 minutes against Belgium and I couldn't tell which team had the red card. It looked like they were playing with this moment in mind, so let's see what they've got. While a draw could get them second, it is very, very unlikely — so they need a win here. I am not a believer, especially after watching their second half against Belgium. I can see a weird scoreline where Iran tries to push in the last 30 minutes, allowing Salah and Marmoush to eat on the counter, but it is more likely that Egypt controls the game for a 2–1 win.

**Prediction: Egypt 2–1 Iran**

## Belgium vs New Zealand

This group is a bit of a mess. The final group to take the field sees Belgium take on New Zealand in Vancouver. This game is interesting because both teams have surprised — Belgium in a bad way, New Zealand in a good way. Belgium's best player has left for the tournament as he and his wife welcome their first child into the world. With all of this being said, I think the jig is up for the Kiwis. Their last half against Egypt exposed some weaknesses, and even without Jeremy Doku, this Belgian team has talent everywhere and should find the gaps that Egypt eventually found late.

**Prediction: Belgium 3–0 New Zealand**
