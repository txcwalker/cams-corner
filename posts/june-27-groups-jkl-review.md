## Phase 3 Review — Groups J, K & L

## Group L

## Panama vs England

England finally got the breakthrough against the low block, sneaking two past Panama in a five-minute span — first from a set piece and then again with a Kane header for a 2–0 win. For England, there were a few lineup changes, one in particular that stood out with Declan Rice being swapped for Morgan Rogers. He looked pretty good, and having two more attack-focused midfielders looked suspicious until the second half when England started creating more chances.

To be fair to England, I think Panama had only two shots the entire game and looked like they were told they weren't allowed to go over the halfway line. They leave pretty unceremoniously, not scoring a single goal, not really threatening offensively. It was just too passive — way, way too passive. They had no skill up top and no plan to score or counter. It looked like they had no plan at all. Back to the drawing board for the Canal Boys.

## Ghana vs Croatia

It was highly likely that both of these teams were going through from the start of the game, with Ghana essentially guaranteed. It was an exciting 2–1 game in favor of Croatia, which was much more even in possession than I anticipated. It looked to me like Ghana was scared or refused to use their speed down the wings until the end. To be clear, Croatia isn't slow on the edge, but that is one of Ghana's main strengths.

Ultimately, this was Croatia showing their class in midfield, with all five of them putting in excellent performances. Modrić had a strong argument for MotM; he played the full 90 minutes and was putting in excellent defensive shifts at the end of the game. He is slowly growing into the tournament.

Unfortunately for our checkered friends, they face Portugal next, and if they win, they will probably get Spain in the Round of 16. Croatia has been to two consecutive semifinals, but the path is steep here. That being said, the team is growing into the tournament as a whole, and they have been better in each match.

As for Ghana, they advance in third place and face Colombia. This was one of the lower-rated teams coming into the tournament, and while I am sure all Ghanaians believed they would get through, no one else did. I really want to see Ghana play some more attacking, enterprising football. They need to use the pace and width they have on the squad and make teams squirm a little bit, at least on the counter. Their best player is Antoine Semenyo, and other than the end of Game 1, he has been largely invisible.

## Group K

## Portugal vs Colombia

Portugal and Colombia was a bit of a snoozefest. The tournament has been low on 0–0 draws, so we needed to help fulfill the quota here. The summary of this game is that neither team could finish, and we had a couple of great saves to keep the game even (plus a very close offside call that was technically correct). Don't have much to say here.

Colombia gets a far more favorable draw for winning the group, getting Ghana and then the winner of Algeria v Switzerland. For Portugal, it's a bit different: they get Croatia in Game 1, then probably Spain in the Round of 16, and if they win that, USA, Belgium, or Senegal. Gross. Portugal only has themselves to blame, dropping the ball against Congo early in the tournament.

## Congo DR vs Uzbekistan

The DRC took that point they earned against Portugal early on and have cashed that into a Round of 32 appearance with the first win in the country's history. They left it late, not scoring until midway through the second half, but were able to nab three goals in their 3–1 victory.

Let's start with Uzbekistan first: they nearly took the lead after just 20 seconds, save for an offside call. Then, just before the 10-minute mark, the Uzbek captain showed one of the best pieces of quality in the tournament with a delicious chip over the goalkeeper. This will be shown across highlight packages for the top goals of the tournament. Tremendous skill, touch, and quality. That was about all that went right for them, as they fell apart in the second half after giving up a penalty.

This gave the Leopards the doorway back into the game that they needed, and they were able to ride the momentum with a go-ahead goal on a brilliant touch about 15 minutes later, followed by one to seal it in stoppage time. All three goals showed the clinical finishing this tournament has been lacking. They get England in the Round of 32, who will be heavily favored. But if the DRC can bring the clinical finishing from this game into that one, England has been known to underperform in big spots before.

## Group J

## Jordan vs Argentina

We saw Argentina sit 9 of 11 starters, including Messi, since they were already through in first place, and they still won 3–1 in convincing fashion over Jordan. They did look decent without Leo, but it is impossible to tell since they are playing such weak competition. That being said, they won't face tough competition until the quarterfinals, with their first game against Cabo Verde (it's been fun, but come on now) and then playing the winner of Egypt v Australia. Even after that, their toughest competition would be Colombia or Switzerland — I mean, it's a complete joke. Anyway, they likely have a few more warmup games before they play any real competition.

For Jordan, in their first World Cup appearance, they scored in all three of their games, including against Argentina, and led against Algeria for a large portion of their match. Yes, they end on 0 points, but it's tough to call this anything other than a smashing success for them. This will hopefully grow the game and soccer culture in Jordan and the surrounding parts of the world. Looking forward to hopefully seeing them back in Spain, Morocco, or Portugal in four years.

## Algeria vs Austria

In possibly the game of the group stage — and the tournament — we had Algeria and Austria showing us exactly what the beautiful game is all about. If you haven't seen how this ends, please go watch and then come back. Fantastic ending to an entertaining game.

This was an evenly matched contest, as the 3–3 scoreline suggests. While there were mistakes on the field, there was also plenty of quality — and we finally got to see two approximately equal sides go head to head. It validates each of their rankings and gives us a real look at their possibilities going forward.

Each team scored in stoppage time, as Austria went from second to eliminated to second in a matter of three minutes of real time. Completely electric atmosphere and an outpouring of emotion in the worst and best way possible — depending on which side you were on. This is what the World Cup is about, on the field and at home. Lovely game to watch.
