## Phase 3 Preview — Groups J, K & L

## Group L

## Panama vs England

The final day of the group stage starts with Group L as Panama and England take over New York for the last time in the group stage, and Croatia take on Ghana in Philly. England are almost certainly through but take on a defensively tough Panamanian team who are unfortunately eliminated. Will England have developed some ideas on how to break down the defensive low block they saw against Ghana and expect to see here? My guess is they will have some new ideas, and time will tell if they pan out.

Often times you need to beat this with one of two things. The first is individual skill down the lines causing the defense to get out of shape and create odd-man situations. The second is having a midfielder or midfielders that can pass through five or six guys and sort of unlock the defense in one swift motion. Both of these require movement off the ball, and while England don't really have the midfielders to do that (few teams do), they certainly have the skill and pace down the line along with the off-the-ball movement.

**Prediction: England 1–0**

## Ghana vs Croatia

For Ghana and Croatia, this is an interesting game. Croatia will almost certainly need a point at minimum to have a chance at advancing. For Ghana, they are probably safe already on 4 points, but they won't want to leave it to chance. I expect them to come out in a similar style to how they played England. The issue here is that Croatia have both the wingers and midfielders to break this kind of defensive setup. Luka Modrić hasn't looked like himself yet, but all it takes is one pass, and they also have Kovačić and even the youngster in Baturina. I think we get one moment of class from Modrić here.

**Prediction: Croatia 1–0**

## Group K

## Portugal vs Colombia

Group K is next up as we have two top-15 teams facing off in Miami as Portugal meet Colombia, while the DRC take on Uzbekistan just up the road in Atlanta. Colombia are already through and are locked into, at worst, second. Portugal have a gaudy goal differential and, even with a loss, should be through as well, but can still technically get third.

So far, both teams have underwhelmed a bit. Yes, Colombia is on 6 points, but they have not looked great in doing it. I expect better from a team that has talent coming out of their ears. Speaking of which, Portugal — we finally saw what we have been waiting to see for seemingly years and several major tournaments. If they continue to play with this chemistry and selflessness, they are going to be a tough out and a tournament favorite. Every position is filled with an All-Star, and depending on the day, their worst player might be Ronaldo. That's not a slight; that is how good this team is.

For Colombia, I need more from the stars. Luis Díaz and James have been fine, average, okay. But we need them to be on fire, player-of-the-tournament performances, and we haven't seen it yet and I am not expecting it here.

**Prediction: Portugal 3–1**

## Congo DR vs Uzbekistan

We have some animal-on-animal action as Les Léopards meet the White Wolves, and we can finally answer the age-old question: cats or dogs? Unfortunately for humanity, the answer is probably cats. Look, it's been a fun ride for Uzbekistan and they are welcome back any time, but they are clearly overmatched in this tournament. In 12 years, when they run the tournament back in the US, this will be a much better team, but they need the soccer culture to grow in the country — for people to prioritize it maybe a little too much, like you get in most of the world. They technically aren't eliminated yet, but they would need to score 8 or 9 goals just to even sniff a chance… it's not happening.

For DR Congo, this is likely a win-and-in scenario. It is really likely that 4 points gets you in, and I expect they have the talent to do it. Despite their loss to Colombia, I thought they were a comparable team, maybe held up a bit by their goalie Mpasi's stellar performance. Looking for Yoane Wissa to show some class in this final game, netting a brace.

**Prediction: Congo DR 3–1**

## Group J

## Algeria vs Austria

The honor of the final games on the final day of group stage play goes to Algeria and Austria in KC, as well as Jordan and Argentina in Dallas. Now we get to the interesting game of the group: the Fennecs and the Peppermint Patties. Both Algeria and Austria have looked bad in this tournament — not just against Argentina, but against Jordan, which is much more concerning. Algeria had about a 30-minute spell where they looked much better than Jordan, but still needed a couple of goalkeeper mistakes to score. This game feels like a dry pool noodle fight, but it could be an interesting battle.

Both teams are looking to win because a loss likely eliminates them, and Austria have draw odds. So while Austria might be okay with a draw, Algeria will not be. 4 points is likely to advance, and they will know exactly what they need at this point.

**Prediction: 2–2 — Algeria look to be the slightly better team, though someone may grab a winner late**

## Jordan vs Argentina

Jordan are unfortunately already eliminated and Argentina are through. I assume Messi will play, but only the first half, though I wouldn't be surprised to see him rested for the whole game. It's been a one-man team really so far, as Messi looks like the only player who can score. It's impossible to evaluate them because they have played two bottom-half teams so far, and shortly a third.

**Prediction: Argentina 3–0**
