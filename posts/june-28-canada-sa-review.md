## Round of 32 Review — Canada vs South Africa

## Canada vs South Africa

I believe in yesterday's preview I said the following:

> *"Overall, all three results are on the table in a game that is going to be tough to watch. However, everyone is going to tune in because it is the first knockout game on the schedule. I expect Canada to win, but not convincingly or easily. It won't be pretty, and it won't make you feel good. Every Canadian fan will have massive anxiety for two-plus hours. Canada 1–0, in a game we will all wish we hadn't watched."*

To literally no one's surprise, this is exactly what we got. Look, this is one of the problems with 32 teams making the knockouts: you are going to get two mediocre teams on their best day playing against each other. And this game was exactly that — except instead it was two below-average teams being bad.

I will give Eustáquio credit; it was a nice two touches in the box and a good finish, especially for a midfielder. Pretty much all of the chances were for Canada in this game, and they missed some big ones throughout. At least they are generating them, but with their finishing ability, they are going to need more in order to score goals and stay in the tournament. To be fair to Canada, there were a couple of goal-line clearances by defenders, but those shots were not struck with particular conviction.

That wasn't their biggest problem, though — their goalie looks completely allergic to catching the ball, and I would not be surprised to see a goalkeeper switch for their next game.

For South Africa, this was a team that was honestly just lucky to be in this position, probably the worst team left in the tournament, and it wasn't particularly close. I think we will see a couple of their players get picked up by some middling European clubs, and hopefully this continues to grow the soccer culture. I will also say I thought their tactics were extremely suspicious, and I can see their coach getting fired despite making it to the knockout rounds. For me, a "process over results" mentality would lead to firing the coach — but we will see, maybe the players love him.
