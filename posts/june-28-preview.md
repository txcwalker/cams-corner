## Round of 32 Preview — Canada vs South Africa

## Canada vs South Africa

The knockout games start today with just one game on the docket, which we have known about for nearly a week as Canada takes on South Africa in LA.

South Africa is in contention for the worst team remaining in the tournament. They looked awful, awful, awful against Mexico to start. They continued that trend until the last 15 minutes of the Czechia game, where they looked… better, and managed a draw. Then, somehow, South Korea came out and looked like they started 11 statues against South Africa, and South Africa barely managed to win that. South Africa scored only two goals in group play, and only one from open play. They love playing the long ball from goal kicks and when the goalie takes possession. Sticking with the theme, they love shooting from outside the box, and I expect that to continue. They haven't looked particularly close to scoring many of those, but Canada's goalie doesn't have the best reputation and has looked particularly suspect in this tournament. As far as talent goes, South Africa does have a bit. Most of their players play in their domestic league, but I wouldn't be shocked to see a couple get some middling contracts from European clubs after their performance.

For Canada, they are slightly below average as far as the teams remaining — somewhere in the low 20s. They have not looked particularly convincing in any one area of the pitch, other than with Ismaël Koné, who is now out with a broken leg. I mentioned the goalkeeping struggles, but they also haven't been strong anywhere else. But, but, but… they scored six goals against Qatar… yeah, a team that played with 9 players for half the game and 10 players for 75% of it. Qatar was one of the worst teams in the tournament. Canada looked uninspired vs. Bosnia and completely useless against Switzerland until the last 20 minutes of the game.

To be fair, they have been missing their best player in Alphonso Davies, who makes a huge difference — probably a bigger difference than Pulisic makes for the US. If he plays, and it looks like he will, this should be no issue for Les Rouges.

Overall, all three results are on the table in a game that is going to be tough to watch. However, everyone is going to tune in because it is the first knockout game on the schedule. Every Canadian fan will have massive anxiety for two-plus hours.

**Prediction: Canada 1–0 — in a game we will all wish we hadn't watched**
