## Round of 32 Review

## Brazil vs Japan

It is really unfortunate to see two top-half teams go up against each other this early in the tournament. Brazil and Japan took the field in Houston, and each win is going to exponentially increase Brazil's chances from here. They are growing in confidence and chemistry, and I do not think we have seen them play anywhere close to their best football yet. They are a deep team with exciting players, youth, and experience. Since their Morocco game, they have looked like the Brazil of old and, most importantly, like they are having fun again, enjoying the game.

I know it was Scotland and Haiti, so let's not get ahead of ourselves. They were bad against a very good Morocco side but were bailed out by Vini Jr. — which is also one of the ways they can win games at this level. Japan had every chance here; this is a very talented team. Their midfield has been one of the better ones at the tournament, and they showed the ability to limit their own mistakes and pounce on the errors of others. They stayed disciplined and organized. But Brazil had too much talent to go out here. Brazil 3–1, adding one late as Japan pushed to equalize and send it to extra time.

## Germany vs Paraguay

Boston hosted Germany and Paraguay for their Round of 32 game, with Germany heavily favored. This is a match you expect to see in the first knockout round: a powerhouse who has looked good in all their games versus a team you forgot was even in the World Cup. Paraguay gave up four goals to the US in their first game — three in the first half — but had yet to concede a goal since then. They play exceptional defense, and all teams knew that coming in. That being said, this German team is the best they have faced, with similar players to Pulisic and better distributors in the middle of the field.

There was a little worry about Germany, as they got progressively worse over their three group games while facing better opponents. Losing to Ecuador is not ideal, especially playing all their starters. Overall, there was too much skill all over the field for Germany and not enough scoring threat from Paraguay — Enciso can't do it all. Germany 3–0, relatively convincingly. The warning sign here is still worth noting: the longer Germany goes without scoring in a given game, the scarier it gets for them — exponentially so, and especially against a defensively disciplined side like this. They cannot let opponents grow in confidence.

## Morocco vs Netherlands

In what could have easily been a semifinal, Monterrey hosted its final game of the World Cup as the Netherlands and Morocco went head-to-head. It is unfortunate that we have to say goodbye to one of these teams — and to the city of Monterrey. They have been lovely hosts, and what a setting for a stadium and event.

Coming into the tournament, Morocco was on the short list of teams that could not only make the final but win it. And now… not so sure. They looked good against Brazil, but not so much against Scotland, and it was up and down against Haiti — more good than bad, but still a lot of missed opportunities. The talent is there: the players, the experience, the tactics, the fans, the support. All of it. But they ran into an absolute buzzsaw in the Dutch.

The Netherlands has one of the best center-back pairings in the tournament. Their strikers have been in form, finishing well and making good runs in the box. Their fullbacks are among the best in the world at providing service. And they have what looks like the best-performing midfield in the tournament — Gravenberch and De Jong have been phenomenal, while Reijnders has played well despite not being first team at Man City. All of this, and we haven't even talked about Summerville coming off the bench. Netherlands 2–0, stamping their mark as championship contenders.
