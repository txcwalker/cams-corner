## Round of 32 Preview

## Ivory Coast vs Norway

Dallas gets to host one of the more exciting games of the Round of 32 — at least on paper — as Côte d'Ivoire takes on Norway. Both teams come in believing they are the better side and have a genuine winning chance. I don't expect either team to sit back and defend the whole game like we saw in all three games yesterday.

The Elephants have a bit more speed down the sides and will look for Yann Diomandé to keep making a difference. He has been taking people on all tournament and causing defenders headaches. He will need to be creative and decisive if Ivory Coast are to come out victorious. They have plenty of other strong players and really looked like the best team in their group from games 1 through 3 — just a miss away from going 3–0.

That being said, they have an interesting matchup ahead. Norway has been playing a 4-3-3 to get their two talented strikers on the field and keep Haaland in the middle where he is best. I would not be surprised to see that change, as Sørloth is not particularly fleet of foot on the right side. We could see a 4-4-2, Sørloth coming off the bench, or maybe just a solo Haaland up top. Hopefully manager Ståle Solbakken won't overcook things like his Dutch counterpart, but they will need to address the speed issue on the edge.

This is a close one, and a game where I think — and maybe hope — we get something back-and-forth with real moments of magic and quality, which have been a little lacking in the knockouts offensively. I like Norway in a close 2–0, but Ivory Coast can absolutely score and win if Diomandé and Diallo are winning 1v1s on the edge and creating in the middle.

**Prediction: Norway 2–0**

## France vs Sweden

Game 2 takes us to New York City as France meets Sweden. This game is best summed up by a bit that was done on the Swedish broadcast the day before the match. They decided to make the best starting eleven from the two teams, combining players from each side to make "Freden" — or maybe "Swance" is better. Of course, the joke is that all 11 players were from the French roster. Instead of Swance or Freden, it's just France. I expect that is how the game will go.

Sweden is best on the attack and their defense is a bit suspect. The issue is that France's attack is the best in the tournament, and their defense is about 10x that of Sweden's. It's hard to imagine the Swedes wanting to sit back and defend — they don't really have the players for it, and they didn't play this way against the Netherlands (although they lost 5–1 there). On the other hand, they don't want to play an open, honest game, giving the French attack time, space, and ease of creativity on the ball. Tough spot.

Looking for France to score early and set the tone. They have too much class to lose here. Olise has been their best player so far but hasn't had a breakout, signature performance yet like Dembélé and Mbappé have. Sweden's best chance is to punch France in the mouth — score not once but twice early on, playing with their best players and burying the two early chances they get. Even then, if France keeps their heads, they are capable of scoring 5 goals on nearly any team. Just give them a reason.

**Prediction: France 4–1**

## Mexico vs Ecuador

The final game of the day takes us back to Mexico City with the battle of the Tris — El Tri vs. La Tri — for a spot in the last 16. So far this tournament, Mexico has looked average at best, with their best half of football being the second half against Czechia. The good news is that was their most recent half. They will have an absolutely raucous crowd behind them, channeling the energy of the whole country in that stadium like they always do.

Mexico needs to figure out their identity, and fast. They have looked like a team without a plan, stumbling into wins by taking advantage of elementary and egregious mistakes from opponents. This must be the worst-looking team to go 3–0 in the group stage and advance in first.

The bad news is they are playing an Ecuador team that does not make many mistakes. They only gave up 5 goals in all of qualifying over 18 games. 18!!! For reference, only 10 teams in Europe gave up fewer, and they played a maximum of 8 games. This is not a team that makes mistakes. They are physical and fast, and they have one of the best ball-winning, holding midfielders in the world in Moisés Caicedo. If he can set the pace and tone, Ecuador will win.

The issue for Ecuador is that the supporting cast — specifically the offensive players — has been all over the place. They only scored 14 goals in qualifying too, and Ecuador played eight 0–0 draws, so they are very comfortable sitting back in tight, ambiguous spots. This game will come down to two things: Mexico playing with a plan, and how effective Ecuador's front six can be.

I'll never predict a 0–0 draw and penalties, but if there were a game for it, it would be this one. I like La Tri over El Tri as host nation Mexico is embarrassed at home.

**Prediction: Ecuador 1–0**
