## Round of 32 Review

## Ivory Coast vs Norway

Three games today, starting with Côte d'Ivoire and Norway in Dallas. This was set up to be one of the more entertaining games of the round, and we got a little bit of everything. Norway came away with a 2–1 win, but I am burying the lede a bit. Finally — and I mean finally — we got to see some of the skill of these players on display. Not the normal impressive stuff like inch-perfect passes or perfectly timed tackles. I am talking about the little moments of magic this tournament so often provides.

The first came from Norwegian winger Antonio Nusa. He scored an absolute worldie, cutting in on his right foot and curling one into the far corner from just inside the box. He made it look so easy, routine even. I can assure you it was not. A tremendous touch to create the inch of space he needed to get the shot away, and you could see from his teammates' reaction that this was not normal for anyone. They had two more chances that were begging to be finished and really should have been up 2–0 at the break.

Ivory Coast's moment of magic didn't come until substitute Amad Diallo entered in the second half and scored what was effectively a solo goal, going through half the Norwegian team on his own. The ball was practically glued to his foot as he glided past defenders, then somehow got the ball in the air as the keeper came out. Completely unreal skill, and despite the quality of the first goal, this one was even more impressive.

Then, of course, the big man up top got his — props go to Oscar Bobb for an incredible pass into the box, and then Berg, who split three defenders to get the ball across to Haaland. Diallo wasn't done, though, forcing an incredible save from 30 yards out in the dying minutes. An electric game with electric players. This is what I want to see at a World Cup.

On the players: Yann Diomandé was incredible all tournament and specifically in this game, causing Norway all sorts of problems on the edges and making something positive feel imminent every time he touched the ball. And Amad Diallo — what can you say? He scored what could end up being the goal of the tournament and then had a beauty of a free kick in the dying minutes. He looked just as good as Diomandé, just in different ways. These two are 19 and 23 respectively. Practically every player on this team is under 30, with a good portion under 25. A very bright future ahead — I'm really looking forward to what they bring to the next couple of World Cups.

For Norway, Patrick Berg and Martin Ødegaard both had excellent games. The manager deserves credit for keeping Berg in after he started on the bench in the first two. Both were calm on the ball, held possession well, pushed the tempo when needed, and got forward at the right moments. The three midfielders were able to cover for each other for pretty much the whole game. For me, Berg was the Man of the Match — always in the right spot, decision-making spot-on, and the decisive assist on a ball that was not easy to square across. Looking forward to Norway vs. Brazil.

## France vs Sweden

Wow. This French team is really, really good. They looked like a total machine today, rolling over Sweden in New York 3–0. Scoreless until Mbappé provided a bit of magic in the 45th minute, Sweden had a couple of chances to equalize in stoppage time before halftime but couldn't get the final touch, and from there it was all France. Sweden looked okay — they were just completely outmatched. The only reason this wasn't 7–0 was Swedish goalkeeper Jacob Widell Zetterström, who was outrageous. His positioning was excellent; he got to pretty much every ball he could and made 9 saves, of which only 2 or 3 were routine. He might have been the best player on the field.

The Swedes exit the tournament able to hold their heads high. A lot to build on, and if they can improve defensively, they have the attacking force to score goals. Viktor Gyökeres is 28, Elanga is 24, Isak is 26, and Ayari is only 22. This looks like a better team than when Zlatan was dragging them to major tournaments.

For France — this team just has it all. Pretty much the entire roster outplayed their counterparts, with the exception of the goalkeeper. Two players to focus on.

Mbappé: I have been critical of him in the past, but he is showing up and showing out right now. 18 World Cup games, 18 goals — absolutely insane. His finishing has been spectacular, netting two today to tie Messi in the Golden Boot race. He is getting 4 or 5 premium chances a game, and while he creates some of them and his positioning and off-the-ball running are elite, for a few of these — including his second today — he is getting passed open by Olise.

Which brings us to Michael Olise. This guy is completely unreal. He is 24 and still improving. He had a bicycle kick attempt from the edge of the box today that hit the post. If that goes in, it is the goal of the tournament, the goal of the year, and probably the goal of the decade. Just the audacity to attempt it is something, and then he hits it pure and literally on frame. He has 5 assists in 4 games — already the most at a World Cup since 1994. He has about 8 elite finishers around him, but he is continually beating multiple defenders with a single pass. His understanding of time and space as it relates to the pace of the game is the best in the tournament. He controls the game and knows it runs through him, which is only possible because his decision-making is basically perfect. Rarely out of position, rarely turning it over in dangerous spots. He is one of my favorite players, so I am biased, but he has been the player of the tournament.

Nearly their entire roster is under 30, including essentially all of their key players. They can run this back in 4 years with just as good — and probably a better — team as new talent emerges. Absolutely disgusting. They face Paraguay next, and even though that team doesn't give up goals… the US scored 4 on them, and this French attack is a smidge better than the Stars and Stripes.

## Mexico vs Ecuador

Boy, was I wrong. Although to be fair, I think Ecuador let a lot of people down today. They got shellacked 2–0, the night capped off by a mouthguard-wearing red card for Hincapié — a good summary of their evening overall. Ecuador was awful; they could hardly create a chance despite reasonable possession. They weren't winning 50/50s, weren't playing with their normal physicality, and for about 10 minutes they completely lost their shape and were in total disarray. That is when Mexico put their two goals on the board. Part of the reason this team only gave up 5 goals in 18 qualifying games is that their stars are all on defense — but those stars did not show up tonight. Caicedo was again pretty much the only player worth anything on the field for them. He got some help from John Yeboah, but 2 vs. 11 goes poorly pretty often.

One interesting note: Enner Valencia, who has been a legend for Ecuador and was their captain at the tournament, handed the armband off to Moisés Caicedo — no surprise there. Valencia was not great at this tournament, and at 36 it feels like he is signaling his international retirement in a passing-of-the-torch moment.

For Mexico, it was definitely the best they have looked yet, but they will need to keep improving to keep winning. It has been 40 years since they have made it past the Round of 16 — the last time was when the World Cup was hosted in Mexico. They get to face the winner of England vs. DRC and will play in the Azteca again. Is that unfair? Yes. Am I glad it is happening? Also yes. It is one of the craziest atmospheres in the sporting world, and those fans are as good as it gets. So far, no issues with the laser pointers or language that have come up in the past. Love the stadium, love the atmosphere — keep it up.

Two players to highlight. Julián Quiñones has been Mexico's best player so far and it is not particularly close — his finishing, movement, and speed are all elite. They need to keep getting him the ball in space. And Gilberto Mora: the 17-year-old became the youngest player to start a knockout game at the World Cup. He looked like he was supposed to be there, played the playmaker role well, and was the best player on the field for the first 10 or 15 minutes before being subbed off after an excellent hour. Keep giving him minutes. I'm really looking forward to his development and can only assume a move to Europe is coming soon.
